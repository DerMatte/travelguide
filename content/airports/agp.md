# AGP Airport Guide

> Focuses on the exact choke points and shortcuts that matter at Málaga during high season.

## Quick Facts
- Single main terminal (T3) handles 90 % of traffic; T2 only for specific Ryanair and seasonal routes.
- No train between terminals; 7-minute covered walkway is the only connection.
- Security is the main bottleneck; no Clear or local fast-track program.
- Summer charter and low-cost surge creates 30-60 min security waits most mornings.
- Currency exchange and SIM cards are cheaper landside than airside.
- Free Wi-Fi is reliable but requires repeated captcha entry; power outlets cluster near gates 40–60 and thin out at the T2 junction.
- Water fountains sit near most T3 restrooms airside; landside Carrefour Express is still the cheapest refill before security.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Liquids must be in 100 ml containers in a single transparent bag; staff are strict.
- Remove laptops and large electronics; trays are limited during rushes.
- Fast-track lanes exist only for business class on Iberia and select Star Alliance flights.
- Arrive before 06:00 or after 21:00 to skip the worst queues.
- Families and passengers with reduced mobility get priority; ask staff at the entrance.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the AENA app and enable gate alerts; it updates faster than airline apps.
- If your flight shows T2, walk the full length of T3 first; the signage is poor at the junction.
- Buy water and snacks landside at the Carrefour Express before security; prices are 30-40 % lower.
- Use the upper departures level for quieter seating when T3 gates are crowded.
- For early flights, the taxi rank at Terminal 3 opens at 05:00; pre-book only if your flight is before 07:00.
- The short-stay car park P1 is directly above check-in; useful for 30-minute drop-offs.
- If you have time between flights, exit airside and re-enter T3; security is often faster on re-entry than internal transfers.
- Keep your boarding pass on your phone; printed copies are rarely requested after security.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T3 is the only terminal most travelers need; follow purple signs.
- T2 is reached via a single moving walkway on level 2; allow 10 minutes including security re-check if required.
- All baggage claim is on the arrivals level of T3; T2 arrivals feed into the same hall.
- Walking time from security to farthest T3 gate is 12-15 minutes.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- The only paid lounge (Sala VIP) is small and often full; skip unless you have Priority Pass.
- Best coffee is at the landside José Luis bakery; airside options are chains only.
- Quiet seating is scarce after 08:00; the far end of T3 near gates 60-80 stays calmer.
- Free charging points are plentiful along the windows but disappear quickly during delays.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Cercanías C1 from the T3 underground station reaches María Zambrano in 8 minutes (~€1.80 zones 1–2 per AENA/Renfe); trains run about every 20 minutes—buy at station machines, not airside kiosks.
- Taxi to city center costs €20-25 with fixed rate; avoid unofficial drivers outside arrivals.
- Short-term parking P1 fills by 09:00 in summer; use P2 or P4 and take the free shuttle.
- Rental cars are located in a separate building; allow 15 extra minutes for the bus transfer.
