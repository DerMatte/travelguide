# BCN Airport Guide

> Practical, no-fluff guide to moving through Barcelona-El Prat quickly and with minimal stress.

## Quick Facts
- Two terminals (T1 and T2) linked by free shuttle bus running every 5-10 minutes.
- Vueling, Ryanair, and easyJet dominate T2; most long-haul and Iberia flights use T1.
- Security is managed by AENA; automated passport gates available for eligible EU/EEA travelers.
- No airside connection between T1 and T2; re-clear security if switching.
- Metro Line 9 Sud reaches both terminals; trains run 05:00-00:00.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Expect 15-40 minutes; mornings before 09:00 and evenings after 18:00 are busiest.
- Liquids in 100 ml containers in a single transparent bag; no PreCheck equivalent.
- Keep passport and boarding pass ready—staff scan both before the queue.
- Families and passengers with reduced mobility get fast-track lanes in T1.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- If flying Vueling or Ryanair from T2, arrive via Metro Line 9 Sud to skip the busier T1 entrance.
- Fill water bottles at the fountains past security in T1 near gates A20 and B30.
- Download the AENA app for live security wait times and gate changes.
- Use the free shuttle only when changing terminals; otherwise stay airside in your departure terminal.
- T2 security is often shorter—switch if your airline operates from both and timing is tight.
- For early flights, check in online and head straight to security; bag drop closes 45 min before departure.
- Quiet seating areas exist airside in T1 near gates A1-A5 and in T2 near gates 70-80.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T1 handles most international traffic; T2 is split into T2A, T2B, and T2C for low-cost carriers.
- Walking time inside T1 from security to farthest gate is about 12-15 minutes.
- Free shuttle between T1 and T2 stops at both landside and airside (airside requires re-clearing).
- Follow yellow “Terminal 2” signs for the bus; it runs 24/7.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- T1 has the best paid dining options post-security; look for Can Vallès and Farga.
- T2 food is limited to chains and vending; eat before security if departing from T2.
- Paid lounges (T1): Can Colomer and the AENA Lounge near gate A29; access via Priority Pass or day pass.
- Free quiet zones with power outlets are available in both terminals near the gates listed above.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Metro Line 9 Sud is fastest and cheapest to city center (35-40 min to Plaça Catalunya).
- Aerobús A1/A2 runs every 5-10 min to Plaça Catalunya (€7.45 one way); avoid during rush hour traffic.
- Official taxi rank is outside arrivals; fixed rate to city center is €39-45 depending on terminal.
- Long-term parking is cheaper at T2; free shuttle to T1 takes 10 minutes.
- Check AENA website for current roadworks or access changes before travel.
