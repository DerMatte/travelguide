# BOG Airport Guide

> Fast, no-nonsense guide to clearing El Dorado with minimal stress and wasted time.

## Quick Facts
- Main hub for Avianca and LATAM Colombia with limited competition on many routes.
- Single terminal building; international arrivals and departures share the same structure.
- Elevation of 8,360 ft means longer takeoff rolls and occasional weight restrictions.
- Rain and fog cause frequent morning and evening delays.
- Free Wi-Fi works but requires frequent re-logins; power outlets are clustered near pier A gates and thin at pier C.
- Water is mostly bottled airside; fill landside if you have time before security.
- Currency exchange and SIM cards available landside before immigration.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Expect full TSA-style screening plus Colombian immigration; PreCheck/Clear not recognized.
- Lines peak 6-10am and 4-8pm; use the far-left security lanes when possible.
- Liquids must be in 100ml containers; no exceptions enforced strictly.
- Have printed or digital boarding pass ready—immigration staff sometimes insist on paper at peak.
- **Connections:** Intl-intl on one Avianca/LATAM ticket usually stays airside via a dedicated transfer security lane (**25–45 min** gate-to-gate practical); intl-domestic requires Colombian immigration and a fresh security pass — budget **45–90 minutes**; domestic-domestic is **5–20 minutes** if gates align.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the Aerocivil app before arrival for live runway and gate data that airlines omit.
- On one ticket, follow "Connecting Flights" signs—intl-domestic still clears Colombian immigration airside but skips baggage reclaim; do not exit to the public arrivals hall.
- Buy a local SIM at the Claro kiosk landside before immigration to avoid inflated airside prices.
- Use the prepaid Taxi Seguro desk inside arrivals; never accept rides from touts in the hall.
- For early flights, sleep in the international departures lounge rather than the crowded check-in hall.
- Carry small-denomination pesos for bus or food vendors; ATMs inside security often run out of cash.
- During rain delays, head to the far end of pier B where seating is usually empty.

## Terminals & Navigation
- All flights use the single terminal; piers A/B handle most international traffic, C/D domestic.
- Allow 12-15 minutes to walk between international and domestic piers.
- Moving walkways frequently break; budget extra time if carrying heavy bags.
- Common mistake: exiting to landside for a connection on one ticket—follow "Connecting Flights" signs instead.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- Crepes & Waffles and Juan Valdez offer reliable local food after security.
- The Avianca lounge in pier A is the only paid lounge worth the fee for long delays.
- Quiet seating exists at the end of pier B near gates 40-50; avoid the central food court.
- Free Wi-Fi is fast enough for video calls but requires frequent re-logins.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Official yellow taxis from the prepaid "Taxi Seguro" desk cost roughly COP 55,000–75,000 (~$14–19) to central zones—cheaper than many US hubs, fixed and safe.
- Uber/DiDi/Cabify operate but use app-directed pickup points (often parking-lot "Aplicaciones" zones, not the main taxi curb)—rideshares are not banned; curbside touts are.
- TransMilenio bus K10 runs to the city but requires a separate ticket and is slow with luggage.
- Short-term parking is expensive; long-term lots fill by 8am on weekdays.
