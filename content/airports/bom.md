# BOM Airport Guide

> This guide cuts through Mumbai airport chaos with the exact moves frequent flyers use to save time and avoid stress.

## Quick Facts
- T1 (domestic) and T2 (international) are separate; free shuttle runs every 15 min but adds 30-45 min total.
- Air India, IndiGo, and Akasa dominate; most international carriers use T2.
- Security is strict on liquids and electronics; expect bag checks even with PreCheck equivalents.
- Peak hours 6-10 AM and 6-10 PM create long lines; monsoon season adds traffic delays.
- **MCT:** Same ticket, int'l–int'l through T2 only — **90 min** floor if bags are through-checked; **T1↔T2** or separate tickets need **2.5–4 h** (shuttle + re-screen).
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free unlimited on **#MumbaiAirportWiFi** — OTP via mobile or passport/boarding-pass voucher at kiosks (T2 gates 48/67/74, level 4 info desks). Speed drops at evening peaks; carry a power bank anyway.
- **Water:** Refill stations post-security in T2; landside level 4 food court before security beats airside bottled prices.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Remove all liquids, electronics, and belts before reaching the scanner—officers will make you repack if missed.
- Domestic security opens 2.5 hours before departure; arrive earlier than that to beat the rush.
- No Clear or TSA PreCheck equivalent; families and elderly get separate (often slower) lanes.
- Avoid 7-9 AM and 7-9 PM windows when both terminals hit maximum queues.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the official CSIA app on cellular before entering; it shows real-time gate changes faster than airline apps.
- Use the T2 level 4 food court pre-security for better, cheaper meals than airside options.
- Book the prepaid taxi from the official counter inside arrivals to skip touts and get fixed rates.
- If connecting T1 to T2, take the free shuttle from the marked bus bay rather than a taxi.
- Carry a printed boarding pass; mobile boarding passes sometimes fail to scan at BOM gates.
- For early flights, stay at the T2 hotel inside the terminal to skip all road traffic.
- Avoid the leftmost security lanes at T2—they move slowest due to older equipment.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T1 and T2 are 4 km apart; the shuttle takes 15-25 minutes depending on traffic inside the airport.
- Domestic-to-international connections require the shuttle and a full security re-screen at T2.
- Walking between T2 check-in and gates can exceed 15 minutes; factor this into tight connections.
- Common mistake: assuming all flights are at T2—double-check your airline terminal the night before.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- T2 has the better food selection; try the Maharashtrian outlets on departures level 4 before security.
- Post-security dining is limited to chains; bring snacks if you have dietary restrictions.
- The T2 Plaza Premium Lounge offers decent showers and quiet space but fills quickly during evening peaks.
- USB outlets exist near select T2 gates (40s, 50s) but are unreliable during banks — charge at Plaza Premium or carry a power bank.
- **Overrated:** Generic airside chains at duty-free prices; **worth it:** Maharashtrian thali landside level 4 pre-security.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Prepaid taxis from the arrivals hall are the most reliable; avoid touts outside.
- Ride-hailing (Uber/Ola) works but requires walking to the designated pickup zone and can add 20 minutes.
- Airport express metro reaches T1 and T2 but is crowded during rush hours; use it only with light luggage.
- Short-term parking at T2 is expensive; long-term lots are cheaper but require a shuttle.
