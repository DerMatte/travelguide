# BUD Airport Guide

> Fast, no-nonsense reference for BUD’s two-terminal layout, low-cost carrier crowds, and direct city links.

## Quick Facts
- Terminals 2A (Schengen) and 2B (non-Schengen) connected by indoor walkway.
- Wizz Air and Ryanair dominate; Lufthansa and Turkish also operate daily.
- Single security checkpoint serves both terminals.
- 100E airport bus is fastest public option to central Budapest.
- Currency exchange and ATMs available pre- and post-security; avoid first row of machines.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Online check-in and bag drop cut wait times dramatically for Wizz and Ryanair.
- Liquids in 100 ml containers only; no PreCheck equivalent.
- Peak congestion 05:30-09:00 and 17:00-20:00; aim outside those windows.
- Shoes stay on; laptops and large electronics must come out.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the bud.hu app for live gate changes and 100E bus times.
- Fill water bottles after security near gate B12; fountains are reliable.
- Use the quiet seating area opposite the langos stand when gates are crowded.
- Buy 100E tickets from the machine outside arrivals to skip driver cash hassle.
- If your flight is delayed, the 2B observation deck offers free views and seating.
- Keep a screenshot of your boarding pass; WiFi occasionally drops during boarding.
- Taxi stands are regulated; never accept rides from inside the terminal.

## Terminals & Navigation
- 2A and 2B share one security hall; after screening, signs direct you left (2A) or right (2B).
- Walking time between farthest gates is under 12 minutes.
- Common mistake: following airline staff to the wrong side of security when gates change.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- Pay-per-use lounges in both terminals are average; skip unless you need showers.
- Langos stand in 2B and the sausage grill in 2A are the only local food worth buying.
- Free charging tables line the windows between security and gates.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- 100E bus (€2.50, every 15-20 min) beats taxis in traffic and cost.
- Official taxi rank uses fixed fares; Budapest city center is €25-30.
- Short-term parking (€3/hour) sits directly outside departures; long-term lots require a free shuttle.
- Train to Nyugati station exists but requires a bus transfer and is rarely faster than 100E.
