# CAI Airport Guide

> Use this page to cut through Cairo’s long queues, confusing terminal layout, and limited post-security options.

## Quick Facts
- Terminal 3 is the main international hub; Terminals 1 and 2 are mostly domestic or low-cost regional.
- EgyptAir operates the majority of flights from Terminal 3; Star Alliance partners use the same facility.
- Security and passport control are sequential and slow; expect 60–120 minutes at peak times.
- **MCT:** T3 int'l–int'l on one ticket — **90 min** floor; experienced flyers use **2 h** at peak (06:00–09:00, 18:00–22:00). T1/T2/T3 changes or separate tickets need **3–4 h** landside.
- No airside people-mover; transfers require walking or infrequent shuttle buses.
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free **CAI-WiFi** (browser login); unreliable near gates — screenshot boarding passes before entering.
- **Water:** Buy landside near T3 check-in; post-security vending is overpriced and often empty — carry an empty bottle if fountains are working (varies by pier).

## Security & Screening Tips
- Remove all electronics and liquids before reaching the first X-ray; agents are strict and re-scans are common.
- Keep passport and boarding pass in hand at all times—multiple checkpoints require them.
- Avoid the 06:00–09:00 and 18:00–22:00 windows when possible; lines move fastest after 22:30.
- No TSA PreCheck or equivalent; all passengers go through the same process.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Change money and buy a local SIM in the arrivals hall before heading upstairs to departures.
- Use the EgyptAir app to check real-time gate numbers; airport screens lag behind.
- Carry a small bottle of water through security—vending machines after security are expensive and often empty. *(Liquid rules are strict; empty bottle + refill is safer if a fountain is available.)*
- If your flight is delayed, exit airside and re-enter; the process is faster than waiting inside.
- Download the Cairo Airport app for offline terminal maps and live flight updates.
- Taxis from the official rank outside arrivals are metered and cheaper than hotel-arranged cars.
- Keep 50–100 EGP in small notes for porters and restroom attendants.

## Terminals & Navigation
- Terminal 3 is split into two piers connected by a long corridor; allow 15 minutes to walk between gates.
- No train or shuttle between terminals once airside; landside bus transfers take 20–30 minutes.
- Most common mistake is arriving at Terminal 1 for an EgyptAir flight—double-check your boarding pass.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- The only reliable food before security is the koshari and ful stands near check-in in Terminal 3.
- Post-security options are limited to a few cafés and a duty-free shop; bring snacks if possible.
- **Overrated:** Paid lounges for walk-ins; **worth it:** koshari/ful landside T3 before immigration.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Official taxis from the rank cost 150–250 EGP to central Cairo; insist on the meter or agree on price first.
- The airport bus to Tahrir Square runs every 30–60 minutes and costs under 20 EGP.
- Short-term parking is available directly in front of Terminal 3; long-term lots are a free shuttle away.
