# CGK Airport Guide

> Practical, no-fluff guide to moving through Jakarta’s main airport with minimal stress and lost time.

## Quick Facts
- Terminals 1 (domestic), 2 (domestic/low-cost), 3 (international + premium domestic)
- Major carriers: Garuda Indonesia, Lion Air, Citilink, AirAsia, Singapore Airlines
- T3 is the newest and most efficient; avoid T1/T2 unless on Lion Group flights
- **MCT:** T3 int'l–int'l on one ticket — **60 min** floor; int'l–domestic or T3↔T1/T2 needs **90 min+** (immigration + landside shuttle).
- Immigration queues often exceed 45 minutes on arrival
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free (browser login via Angkasa Pura portal); drops during evening banks — download the app on cellular first.
- **Water/power:** T3 has working outlets at most gates; T1/T2 outlets are mostly dead. Buy water landside T3 before security.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Remove liquids and laptops at T3; bins are scarce and staff are strict.
- Premium passengers and Garuda business class get a separate fast-track lane—use it.
- Avoid 05:30-08:30 and 19:00-22:00; lines double during these windows.
- No TSA PreCheck equivalent; bring printed boarding pass as mobile signal drops.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the Angkasa Pura 2 app before landing for live gate and delay info.
- Use the free shuttle bus between terminals instead of taxis; it runs every 15 minutes.
- Landside food court in T3 (near check-in row 5) has better and cheaper local food than airside.
- If connecting to a domestic flight, clear immigration first then re-check bags at T3.
- Grab a Gojek or Grab at the official pickup zone on level 1 arrivals; ignore touts.
- T3 has working power outlets at most gates; T1 and T2 outlets are mostly dead.
- Carry small Rupiah notes for bottled water and SIM cards landside.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T3 handles almost all international flights and Garuda domestic; walking time between gates rarely exceeds 10 minutes.
- T1 and T2 are older, poorly signed, and require a 20-30 minute shuttle or taxi ride from T3.
- No moving walkways between terminals; budget 45 minutes for any T3-to-T1/T2 connection.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- T3 has the only decent lounges: Garuda Business Lounge (paid access available) and the new Plaza Premium.
- Best quick meal landside: sate or bakso stalls opposite check-in counters.
- Quietest seating is the far end of T3 departures near gates 1-5; avoid the central food court.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- DAMRI bus to central Jakarta is cheapest (Rp 40,000) but slow in traffic.
- Grab/Gojek from the official zone is fastest and costs Rp 150,000-250,000 to the city.
- Official taxi stands are reliable; insist on the meter or fixed airport rate.
- Short-term parking is expensive and fills quickly; only use if picking up.
