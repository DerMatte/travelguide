# CLT Airport Guide

> Fast reference for the single-terminal American hub that rewards knowing the walkway layout and checkpoint timing.

## Quick Facts
- American Airlines operates the vast majority of flights; other carriers use same terminal.
- One security checkpoint serves all concourses; no satellite terminals.
- Concourses run A-B-C-D-E in a straight line; walking times 3-15 minutes.
- Free cell phone lot sits 2 minutes from arrivals; short-term garage directly above.
- TSA PreCheck and CLEAR lanes are on the far left after entering the checkpoint.
- No airside people-mover; plan extra time for A-E connections.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Main checkpoint opens at 4 a.m.; arrive by 5 a.m. for first bank.
- PreCheck line is almost always shorter than standard after 7 a.m.
- CLEAR is useful only during 6-8 a.m. and 4-6 p.m. rushes.
- Avoid the checkpoint 5:30-7 p.m. when evening banks and arriving international passengers overlap.
- Liquids in quart-size bags are still enforced; no known relaxed policy.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Park in the short-term garage level 3-4 for fastest walk to security; works best when staying under 4 hours.
- Use the return-side moving walkway after security to reach distant gates faster; avoid if heading to A gates.
- Grab food in the Atrium before security if your connection is under 45 minutes; lines are shorter than airside.
- Cell phone lot has a digital board showing arrivals; pull in only when the flight shows “landed.”
- For same-terminal connections under 35 minutes, stay airside and walk briskly; re-clearing adds 20+ minutes.
- Charge devices at the power stations near gates D/E; they are less crowded than the Atrium.
- Download both the CLT and American apps; gate changes appear here first during weather holds.
- If renting a car, take the shuttle from the rental center instead of walking the long garage path in summer heat.

## Terminals & Navigation
- Single terminal with five concourses in linear order; all connections on foot.
- A to E takes 12-15 minutes at normal pace; add 5 minutes with bags or mobility issues.
- No train or tram; moving walkways exist only on the post-security return corridor.
- Common mistake: exiting security for a connection; you must re-clear and lose 25-40 minutes.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- The Atrium before security offers the widest seating and reliable options (Chick-fil-A, local coffee).
- Concourse D/E has the best post-security variety; quieter seating near E gates after 8 p.m.
- No Centurion Lounge; American Admiral’s Club is the main paid option near the Atrium.
- Water refill stations sit next to most restrooms; carry an empty bottle through security.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Short-term garage is the fastest for pick-up/drop-off; daily max rate applies after 24 hours.
- Cell phone lot is free and 2 minutes away; use when driver is meeting arrivals.
- Rental car shuttle runs every 5-7 minutes from the terminal; allow 15 minutes total.
- Rideshare pickup is on the lower arrivals level; follow signs to avoid circling.
