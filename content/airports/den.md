# DEN Airport Guide

> Practical, time-saving guide to moving through Denver International Airport — security is now consolidated on Level 6 with CT scanners.

## Quick Facts
- One **Jeppesen Terminal** feeds **Concourses A, B, C** via underground train (**5–8 min** rides) from **Level 4** after security.
- **Concourse A** can also be reached airside via the **A-Bridge** from **Level 6** (reopened Aug 2025 with the East checkpoint).
- **United** dominates **Concourse B**; **Frontier** and **Southwest** use **A** and **C**.
- **Security (2025–2026):** All screening is on **Level 6** — **West** (opened 2024) and **East** (opened **5 Aug 2025**) checkpoints, each with **17 lanes**, **CT X-ray**, and automated lanes ([TSA DEN release](https://www.tsa.gov/news/press/releases/2025/08/04/tsa-begin-operations-new-east-security-checkpoint-den)). Legacy Level 5 checkpoints are **closed**.
- CT lanes generally allow **laptops and compliant liquids to stay in bags** — still follow officer directions.
- Free airport Wi-Fi is reliable; long gate walks remain common (**20–30 min** between distant gates).

## Security & Screening Tips
- Use **Level 6** only — **West** near United counters, **East** near Southwest; both open **03:00–01:00** daily per TSA.
- **CLEAR + PreCheck** combo is fastest; peak screening **04:00–08:00**.
- Arrive **90 min** early domestic, **2.5 h** international; add **30 min** during snow ops.
- Do not follow outdated advice to use **Level 5** or the old **east checkpoint after 8 p.m.** — those facilities are retired.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **United (Concourse B)?** West checkpoint often aligns with your gate flow; **Southwest (C)?** East checkpoint can be quicker.
- Board the train at the far end of the platform during peaks to grab a seat.
- If connecting **B ↔ C**, riding to **A** and transferring can beat a packed direct train — only when displays show delays.
- Download the **flydenver** app for train and **Level 6** security wait times.
- Park in the economy lot and take the free shuttle when returning after **10 p.m.** — beats garage rates.
- The **meditation room** near **Gate A34** is rarely used and has power outlets.

## Terminals & Navigation
- All passengers check in and clear security in **Jeppesen**, then take **Train to Gates** on **Level 4** (or **A-Bridge** to **A**).
- Walking between **B** and **C** without the train adds **25+ min** and stairs — do not attempt.
- Baggage claim is on **Level 5**; allow **15 min** from train arrival.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- **United Club (B)** is the largest and least crowded after **8 p.m.**
- **DEN Market** in the terminal has solid grab-and-go sandwiches and local beer.
- **Great Hall** food hall offers better variety than many concourse options — eat before boarding the train when time allows.
- Quietest seating: upper level near tarmac windows in the terminal.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **RTD A Line** to Union Station costs **$10.50** and runs every **15–30 min**; fastest public option ([RTD Airport](https://www.rtd-denver.com/airport/)).
- Rideshares pick up on **Level 5**; expect **10–20 min** wait during peak arrival banks.
- Economy parking is **$15/day** with free shuttle every **10 min**; garage is **$32/day** but closer.
- Snow delays hit the **A Line** hardest — have a backup rideshare ready in winter.
