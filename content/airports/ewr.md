# EWR Airport Guide

> Practical, no-fluff guide to moving through Newark Liberty International Airport quickly and with minimal stress.

## Quick Facts
- Three terminals (A, B, C) connected by free AirTrain running every 2-3 minutes.
- United Airlines dominates Terminal C; most other carriers use A or B.
- AirTrain links directly to NJ Transit and Amtrak at Newark Airport station for Manhattan access.
- TSA PreCheck and CLEAR lanes operate in all terminals; CLEAR is fastest at peak times.
- International arrivals handled in B or C; baggage claim and customs can add 45-90 minutes.
- Terminal A redevelopment ongoing; some gates and walkways may be closed.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Terminal C PreCheck line is usually shortest but still backs up 7-10 a.m. and 4-7 p.m.
- CLEAR members should use the dedicated lanes in C near the United check-in counters.
- Avoid Terminal B security midday when international arrivals create secondary surges.
- Liquids in quart-size bags only; no known EWR-specific exceptions.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the United app and enable gate-change alerts; EWR reassignments happen often.
- Take AirTrain to Terminal C even from other terminals for better food and lounge access if flying United.
- Refill water bottles at the stations near C111 or C70 after clearing security.
- Use the NJ Transit ticket machines on the AirTrain platform level to avoid station queues.
- If connecting from an international arrival in B to a domestic United flight, stay airside and take AirTrain rather than re-clearing security.
- Park in P4 for Terminal C when possible; it has the shortest covered walk to check-in.
- Check the Port Authority site the night before for any AirTrain or rail service alerts.

## Terminals & Navigation
- AirTrain is the only practical way to move between terminals; walking paths are long and exposed.
- Terminal C to B transfer takes about 8 minutes on AirTrain; add 10 minutes for walking to gates.
- Common mistake: exiting security to change terminals, which forces full re-screening.
- Terminal A is smallest and easiest to navigate but has fewer amenities.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- United Club in Terminal C (near C74 and C120) offers decent food and showers; access via membership, day pass, or Star Alliance status.
- Terminal C has the strongest food options post-security, including Shake Shack and local spots.
- Quietest seating is usually near gates C130-C140 when not crowded.
- No standout paid lounges worth the cost for most travelers; United Club is the only consistent option.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- NJ Transit to New York Penn Station is fastest and cheapest rail option; trains run frequently.
- AirTrain to Newark Airport station is free; buy NJ Transit tickets at machines before boarding.
- Taxi or rideshare pickup is on the lower level; expect 20-40 minute waits at peak evening hours.
- Parking: P4 is closest for Terminal C; economy lots require bus transfer and add time.
