# FCO Airport Guide

> Practical, time-saving intel for the airport that handles most visitors to Rome.

## Quick Facts
- **Terminals:** **T1** (domestic/LCC), **T3** (main international), **T5** (US-only — shuttle from T3).
- **MCT (same ticket, practical floor):**
  - **T3 → T3, Schengen ↔ non-Schengen:** **60 min** floor, **90 min** comfortable — passport control at pier root.
  - **T3 → T5 (US departures):** **90 min** floor — bus from T3 plus US secondary screening in T5.
  - **T1 ↔ T3:** **75 min** floor via covered walkway.
  - **Separate tickets:** **2.5–3 h** minimum.
- **Rail:** **Leonardo Express** to Roma Termini every **15 min** (**32 min**, **€14**).
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free but captcha every **~45 min** — annoying on long layovers.
- **Water:** Refill fountains in T3 near train-station level and gates **D1–D10** / **E31–E40**; bring an empty bottle.
- **Power:** Charging tables near gates **D1–D10** and **E31–E40** — bring your own cable.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Non-Schengen and US flights share the same security area after check-in; expect 30-50 min at peak.
- Use the ADR app to pick the shortest lane; lanes 4-6 are usually fastest mid-morning.
- Liquids in 100 ml bags are strictly enforced; no exceptions at T3.
- Families and passengers with reduced mobility get fast-track lanes—ask staff at entrance.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the ADR app before arrival; it shows real-time security wait times and gate changes.
- If your flight is delayed, stay landside in T3 near the train station—better food and seating.
- For early morning departures, check in online and go straight to security; bag drop opens 2 h prior.
- US-bound passengers: clear Italian exit immigration in T3 before the T5 shuttle to avoid the longest line.
- Keep your boarding pass on your phone; paper copies are rarely requested after security.
- The quietest seating is the upper level of T3 near gates E31-E40; power outlets are plentiful.
- Buy a Trenitalia ticket from the machines in arrivals if heading to other Italian cities—faster than the airport desk.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T3 is the central hub; T1 is a short covered walkway away; T5 requires a 7-minute bus from T3.
- Minimum connection time between Schengen and non-Schengen is 60 min; allow 90 min if bags are checked through.
- Follow yellow signs for “Partenze” (departures) and blue for arrivals; English is limited on older signs.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- The best paid lounge is the ITA Lounge in T3 (airside) for €45 day pass—good showers and quiet zones.
- Landside: All’Arrivo in arrivals has excellent coffee and sandwiches; airside choices are mostly chains.
- Free charging tables are located near gates D1-D10 and E31-E40; bring your own cable.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Leonardo Express train leaves every 15 min from T3 basement; 32 min to Termini, €14 one-way.
- Terravision or SIT buses are cheaper (€7-8) but take 45-60 min and stop at multiple hotels.
- Short-term parking (P1-P4) costs €5 per hour; long-term P5-P7 is €12 per day if booked online.
- Taxi flat rate to central Rome is €48 (fixed, includes luggage); avoid touts outside arrivals.
