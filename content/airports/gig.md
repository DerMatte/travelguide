# GIG Airport Guide

> Practical, no-fluff guide to moving through Galeão quickly and avoiding the most common pain points.

## Quick Facts
- **Terminals:** **T1** (most domestic), **T2** (international + select domestic) — **8–10 min** covered walkway.
- **MCT (same ticket, practical floor):**
  - **Same terminal, domestic → domestic:** **60 min** floor.
  - **T1 ↔ T2 (one ticket):** **90 min** floor — walkway plus potential re-security.
  - **International → domestic:** Immigration dominates — **2 h** minimum.
  - **Separate tickets:** **3 h** minimum (bags, re-check, immigration).
- **Distance:** **20 km** north of Copacabana; **Linha Vermelha** traffic can double drive time.
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free throughout both terminals — slow during morning peaks; buy a local SIM at T2 arrivals for Uber.
- **Water:** Airside fountains sparse — T2 gates **30–40** have the most reliable outlets; carry water after security.
- **Power:** T2 gates **30–40** and **50–60** end — charge before the food-court crush after **20:00**.
- **BRT Expresso Aeroporto:** Every **10–20 min**, **R$7.50** to downtown/Barra.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Expect 40–60 min at peak; use the priority lane if flying business or holding Star Alliance Gold.
- Remove liquids and electronics early—bins are limited and agents are strict.
- Avoid the far-left lanes in T2; they move slowest.
- PreCheck/Clear equivalents do not exist; arrive early instead.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the official RioGaleão app before arrival for live gate and BRT updates.
- After landing, exit T2 arrivals and walk 3 min to the BRT stop instead of queuing for taxis.
- If connecting from T1 to T2, use the free shuttle when it rains—the walkway has no cover.
- Buy a prepaid SIM at the T2 arrivals stand; it’s faster than hunting Wi-Fi for Uber.
- For early flights, clear security the night before if staying at the nearby Windsor or Novotel.
- T2 airside has the only reliable power outlets near gates 30–40; charge devices there.
- Skip the official taxi desk; Uber and 99 are 30–40 % cheaper and wait in the same curb.
- Keep a small amount of cash for BRT; card machines on buses often fail.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T2 is the newer, cleaner terminal—use it for international departures and arrivals.
- Walking time T1 to T2 is 8–10 min; allow 15 min with bags or mobility issues.
- Most common mistake: following signs to “international departures” from T1 and ending up outside.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- Star Alliance Lounge in T2 airside is the best paid option; food is solid but crowded.
- Good casual eats are in T2 after security: juice bars, açaí stands, and pastel counters.
- Quietest seating is near gates 50–60 in T2; avoid the central food court after 8 p.m.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- BRT Expresso Aeroporto is fastest and cheapest to South Zone and Barra.
- Uber pickup is on the departures level curb—ignore drivers inside arrivals.
- Short-term parking is expensive; long-term lots fill by 7 a.m. on weekdays.
- Allow 90 min buffer to downtown during rush hour (7–10 a.m. and 5–8 p.m.).
