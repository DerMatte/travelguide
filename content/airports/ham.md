# HAM Airport Guide

> Fast, low-stress guide for the two-terminal Hamburg hub with reliable rail links and predictable security peaks.

## Quick Facts
- **Terminals:** **1** and **2** linked by **5-min** indoor walkway on level 2 — most connections stay airside.
- **MCT (same ticket, practical floor):**
  - **Same terminal, Schengen:** **45 min** floor, **60 min** comfortable.
  - **T1 ↔ T2, Schengen:** **60 min** floor — central security re-screen if you crossed zones wrong.
  - **Non-Schengen involved:** **75–90 min**.
  - **Separate tickets:** **2 h** minimum.
- **Security:** Single **central checkpoint** in Airport Plaza between T1/T2 ([HAM security](https://www.hamburg-airport.de/de/abfliegen-ankommen/bundespolizei/sicherheitskontrolle)) — peaks **05:30–08:00** and **16:00–19:00**.
- **Slot & Fly:** Free reserved security window up to **72 h** before departure — worth booking on Monday/Friday mornings ([HAM travel tips](https://www.hamburg-airport.de/en/plan-book/travel-tips-info-for-a-relaxed-start-to-your-vacation-36222)).
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free unlimited, no login — genuinely reliable.
- **Water:** Fountains just past central security in both terminals — bring a refillable bottle.
- **Power:** Charging areas near T1 gates **C–D** upper level; Vielfliegertreff consensus: Plaza-side lanes beat the central crush at **06:00–08:00**.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Arrive at security 90 minutes before Schengen departures; 120 minutes for non-Schengen.
- Use the automated gates if you hold an EU passport or registered ID.
- Avoid the central security area 5:30-8:00 and 16:00-19:00; use the side lanes when open.
- Liquids must be in a single transparent bag; no exceptions at this airport.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Use **Slot & Fly** to book a free security window — skips the worst Plaza waves (official HAM service; FF reports confirm it beats arriving early and queuing).
- Landside bakery **Hauptwache** serves better coffee and sandwiches than any airside outlet.
- If your flight is delayed, walk to the quieter Terminal 2 departures area for seating.
- S-Bahn machines accept contactless cards; skip the ticket counter queue.
- Free charging lockers are located near gate areas in Terminal 1, level 2.
- For early flights, the Parkhaus P4 drop-off is closest to security and rarely congested.
- Check the departure screens at the top of the escalators; they update faster than gate monitors.
- Bring a refillable bottle; water fountains sit just past security in both terminals.

## Terminals & Navigation
Terminals 1 and 2 sit side-by-side with a five-minute covered walkway on level 2. Most Eurowings flights depart from Terminal 1; Condor long-haul uses Terminal 2. Walking times between farthest gates rarely exceed 12 minutes. Follow yellow “Umsteigen” signs for connections; no bus or train ride is required airside.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
The Lufthansa Lounge in Terminal 1 is the only paid lounge worth considering for non-status passengers. For food, Hauptwache landside offers superior baked goods and seating. Airside, the best quick option is the Nordsee fish counter near gate D. Quiet seating areas with power outlets are located between gates C and D on the upper level.

## Ground Transport & Parking
S-Bahn S1 is the fastest and cheapest route to the city center. Taxis cost €35-40 and take 20-30 minutes depending on traffic. Short-term parking P1/P4 sits directly in front of the terminals; long-term parking is cheaper via the shuttle from P8. Book P8 online in advance for stays over three days.
