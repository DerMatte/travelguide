# HEL Airport Guide

> One terminal, fast rail link, and predictable security make HEL one of Europe's least stressful major airports when you know the patterns.

## Quick Facts
- Single terminal with clear Schengen/non-Schengen split after security.
- Finnair operates the majority of long-haul flights; codeshares dominate gates 30-55.
- Ring Rail Line (I and P trains) runs every 10 minutes to Helsinki Central Station.
- Free Wi-Fi is fast and stable throughout the building.
- Showers, nap pods, and a small sauna are available airside near gate 28.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Use the dedicated fast track if you hold Finnair Plus Platinum or oneworld Emerald.
- Avoid the leftmost lanes 06:30-08:30; they serve early long-haul flights and move slower.
- Liquids in 100 ml containers are strictly enforced; no exceptions at this airport.
- Families and passengers with reduced mobility get priority lanes—ask staff at the entrance.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the Finavia app before arrival; it shows real-time security wait times by lane.
- If your connection is under 50 minutes, stay airside—re-clearing security is rarely worth it.
- Free showers at gate 28 are rarely busy after 10:00; bring your own towel or buy one at the pharmacy.
- The quiet zone near gate 22 has power outlets and dim lighting; ideal for layovers over two hours.
- Buy a Helsinki City Card at the tourist desk if you have a long layover—covers the train and many museums.
- Non-Schengen passengers can use the tax-refund machines immediately after security to skip the queue at departures.

## Terminals & Navigation
- Everything is under one roof; follow overhead signs for Schengen (gates 1-29) or non-Schengen (gates 30-55).
- Walking time from security to farthest gate is 12-14 minutes with moving walkways.
- Buses to remote stands depart from the lower level; allow an extra 10 minutes.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- Kitchen & Co (pre-security) offers the best value salmon plates and quick service.
- Olo Bar airside serves excellent Finnish craft beer and small plates with runway views.
- The Finnair Lounge (non-Schengen) is solid for oneworld Sapphire and above; buffet is replenished frequently.
- Free charging tables and quiet seating are scattered near gates 20-25.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Ring Rail Line is fastest and cheapest to the city center (€5.50, 27 min).
- Finnair City Bus runs every 20 minutes when rail is disrupted (€6.90).
- Short-term parking P1/P2 is expensive; use P3 or P4 for stays over four hours and take the free shuttle.
