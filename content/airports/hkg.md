# HKG Airport Guide

> Fast, no-fluff reference for clearing HKG with minimal stress and wasted time.

## Quick Facts
- One integrated terminal; Midfield Concourse reached by 5-minute automated people mover.
- Cathay Pacific dominates; secondary bases for HK Express and Greater Bay Airlines.
- Combined immigration + security; e-Channel available after online enrollment for frequent visitors.
- Free, fast Wi-Fi everywhere; Octopus card covers Airport Express, buses, and MTR.
- Lounges are limited after 10 p.m.; only The Wing and Plaza Premium stay open late.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Expect 25-40 minutes at peak (07:00-10:00 and 18:00-21:00); liquids in 100 ml rule strictly enforced.
- Enroll in e-Channel online before travel to skip manual immigration lines on future visits.
- Remove laptops and large electronics only at the X-ray; keep shoes on unless flagged.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the official HKG app for live gate changes; it updates before most airline apps.
- Use the free shower rooms in the Wing lounge (pay for day pass) if you have a long layover.
- Buy an Octopus card at the MTR station inside arrivals for seamless Airport Express + city travel.
- For same-terminal connections under 50 minutes, stay airside and ride the people mover; do not exit.
- Grab a Citybus A21 or A22 from the ground floor if Airport Express is full or delayed.
- The quietest seating is near gates 40-60 after security; most crowds cluster around food courts.
- If your flight is delayed past midnight, the 24-hour Plaza Premium lounge near gate 60 is the only reliable option.

## Terminals & Navigation
- Everything is under one roof; follow overhead signs for “Gates 1-80” or “Midfield Concourse.”
- Walking time from security to farthest gate is 12-15 minutes; people mover adds 5 minutes to Midfield.
- Common mistake: exiting immigration for a connection—re-entry requires new security screening.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- The Wing (Cathay) and Plaza Premium offer the best paid day passes; food quality is consistent.
- Landside Tim Ho Wan serves excellent dim sum before security; airside options are smaller and close earlier.
- Free showers exist in both paid lounges; no free public showers after security.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Airport Express reaches Central in 24 minutes; buy tickets at machines or use Octopus.
- City buses A10/A11/A21 are cheaper and run 24 hours; use for Kowloon or Hong Kong Island.
- Short-term parking is expensive; long-term lots require a free shuttle that runs every 15 minutes.
