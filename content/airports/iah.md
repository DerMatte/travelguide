# IAH Airport Guide

> One concise page of the exact moves that cut wait times and navigation errors at Houston’s main airport.

## Quick Facts
- **Terminals:** **A–E** — United hub (**A, B, C, E**); **D** primary international arrivals.
- **MCT (same ticket, practical floor — [Fly2Houston](https://www.fly2houston.com/iah/related-content/is-houston-a-good-airport-to-connect-through/)):**
  - **Domestic → domestic (Skyway airside):** **45–60 min** floor, **75 min** comfortable.
  - **Domestic → international:** **55–75 min** floor.
  - **International → domestic:** CBP at **D/E** — **90 min–2 h** minimum.
  - **Separate tickets:** **2.5–3 h** minimum.
- **Skyway:** Airside train every **~2 min**, **A → D/E ~4 min** — no re-screen when staying airside.
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free **IAH Free WiFi** — registration required; fine for gate tracking.
- **Water:** Bottle-fill fountains in **E** near **The Grove** and **D** gate clusters — sparse elsewhere.
- **Power:** Terminal **E** far end (**E24**) and **D6** yoga room area — quieter than central **C**.
- **PreCheck** all terminals; **CLEAR** only **A** and **E**.

## Security & Screening Tips
- PreCheck lanes open in every terminal; use the United app to see real-time wait times.
- Peak congestion hits 6–9 a.m. and 3–6 p.m. on weekdays—arrive outside those windows when possible.
- CLEAR is fastest at Terminal E; skip it at A if the line exceeds five people.
- Liquids in carry-on are still enforced; no special IAH exemptions.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the United app and enable gate-change alerts; IAH gates flip frequently.
- If connecting from D to any other terminal, take the train one stop instead of walking the tunnel.
- Terminal E has the newest restrooms and shortest post-security lines after 8 p.m.
- Use the free cell-phone lot on JFK Blvd; it’s a 4-minute drive back to arrivals.
- For early flights, park in Terminal C garage and walk to A/B—often faster than the shuttle.
- Need a quiet gate? Walk to the far end of E near gate E24; foot traffic drops sharply.
- Rideshare pickup at Terminal C is usually 3–5 min faster than A or B during rush hours.

## Terminals & Navigation
- A–B–C–E are connected by train; D requires a short walk or train ride from E.
- Average train ride between any two terminals is under 8 minutes.
- Common mistake: following signs for “international arrivals” when departing domestic—stay on the departures level.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- United Club in Terminal E (near E8) is the largest and least crowded.
- The Grove (E) and Pappadeaux (E) are the two reliable sit-down spots past security.
- Terminal D has a small yoga room near gate D6 that is almost always empty.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Uber/Lyft pickup is on the lower level of Terminal C; add 5–7 min buffer during 4–7 p.m.
- METRO 102 bus to downtown is $1.25 and runs every 20–30 min; use it when traffic is heavy.
- Terminal C garage is the closest covered parking to most United gates; book in advance via the airport site.
