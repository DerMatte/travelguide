# ICN Airport Guide

> Fast, no-nonsense reference for clearing ICN with minimal stress — and picking the right terminal after the 2026 Asiana move.

## Quick Facts
- **Terminals 1 and 2** are **not** walkable; the free **inter-terminal shuttle** runs about every **10 min**, **05:00–23:00**, **~15–18 min** ride ([Incheon Airport map](https://www.airport.kr/ap/en/map/mapInfo.do?SN=1552)).
- **Terminal 2 (from Jan 2026):** **Korean Air**, **Asiana Airlines** (moved **14 Jan 2026**), **Jin Air**, **Air Busan**, **Air Seoul**, and most **SkyTeam** partners (Delta, Air France, KLM, etc.) — confirm on your boarding pass, not alliance alone ([Asiana notice](https://flyasiana.com/C/KR/KO/customer/notice/detail?id=CM202509300002526502)).
- **Terminal 1:** Most other international carriers — **JAL**, **ANA**, many LCCs, and remaining **Star Alliance** members that did not move to T2.
- **MCT (practical):** Same terminal **60–75 min** floor; **T1 ↔ T2** treat **90 min** as minimum, **2 h** safer in peaks (shuttle + walking + possible re-screening).
- **Separate tickets / self-transfer:** **3+ hours** minimum between terminals.
- Security and immigration are efficient but slow during **06:00–09:00** and **18:00–22:00** peaks.
- Free Wi-Fi is reliable; power outlets are plentiful at gates.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Expect full liquids screening; **100 ml** rule strictly enforced.
- No TSA PreCheck equivalent; arrive **2.5 h** early for international flights during peaks.
- **T2** generally has newer equipment and shorter lines for Korean Air / Asiana banks — still queue at morning departures.
- Avoid the central security area in **T1** between **07:00–08:30**; side lanes can move quicker when open.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **Verify terminal on your ticket** — Asiana and subsidiaries no longer use T1 as of **January 2026**; old habits misroute thousands of passengers.
- Download the official airport app for real-time shuttle and gate info before landing.
- If connecting **T1 ↔ T2**, use the **free shuttle** from the **3rd floor** departure hall (not a landside taxi unless you have hours to spare).
- **Transit hotel / capsule** between terminals lets you shower and nap without clearing Korean immigration (eligibility rules apply).
- Buy a **T-money** card at AREX machines for seamless bus/metro rides into Seoul.
- **Korean Air lounge (T2)** offers better food and showers than most paid options for eligible passengers.
- For early flights, the **24-hour rest zone on T2 Level 4** beats fighting for gate seating.
- Skip taxi queues at peak arrival times if you can reach the **bus level** for Airport Limousine departures.

## Terminals & Navigation
- **T1** and **T2** are **2 km apart**; walking is not practical — always use the shuttle.
- Each terminal has its own **concourse train** to remote gates after security — check both terminal and gate pier.
- **Common mistakes:** Going to T1 for Asiana; assuming alliance = terminal; booking **60 min** T1↔T2 connections on separate tickets.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- **Best lounge:** Korean Air First/Business in **T2** — showers, quality Korean food, quiet zones when not in a bank.
- **Solid paid option:** The Lounge in **T1** (near gate 40) for non-Korean Air passengers.
- **Standout food:** Korean BBQ and bibimbap counters on **T2 departures (4F)**; avoid overpriced global coffee chains at peak.
- **Quietest spot:** T2 rest zone on the **4th floor** near the transit hotel.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **AREX express** is fastest and cheapest to **Seoul Station** (**₩9,500**, **~43 min**); runs every **15–30 min** ([AREX](https://www.arex.co.kr/en/)).
- KAL limousine buses are comfortable for specific hotel drops but slower in traffic.
- Taxis to central Seoul cost **₩60,000–80,000**; use the official stand to avoid overcharges.
- Short-term parking is expensive; long-term lots are cheaper but require shuttle.
