# ICN Airport Guide

> Fast, no-nonsense reference for clearing ICN with minimal stress and wasted time.

## Quick Facts
- **Terminals 1 and 2** are separate buildings connected by an **internal shuttle train** (not walkable). Shuttle runs every ~5 min; airside T1↔T2 transfers require connection security.
- **Terminal 2 (from Jan 2026):** Korean Air, **Asiana Airlines** (relocated 14 Jan 2026), Jin Air, Air Busan, Air Seoul, and SkyTeam partners (Delta, Air France, KLM, etc.).
- **Terminal 1:** Most remaining Star Alliance carriers (ANA, Singapore Airlines, Lufthansa, etc.), oneworld, and other foreign/LCC airlines. **Asiana is no longer here** despite still being a Star Alliance member on paper.
- **Critical:** Do not guess terminal by alliance — check your **flight number** on the airport website or app.
- Security and immigration are efficient but slow during 06:00–09:00 and 18:00–22:00 peaks.
- Free Wi-Fi is reliable; power outlets are plentiful at gates.
- AREX, buses, and taxis all depart from the arrivals level of each terminal.

## Security & Screening Tips
- **Shoe removal (since Dec 2024):** At **cylindrical body scanners** (~70% of lanes), passengers wearing shoes with heels **≥3.5 cm** (including platform slippers, boots, and rain boots) must remove them. Sneakers and flat shoes are exempt. Walk-through metal detectors do not require shoe removal.
- Expect full liquids screening; 100 ml rule strictly enforced.
- No PreCheck equivalent; arrive 2.5 h early for international flights during peaks.
- T2 security lanes are generally newer; T1 Concourse gates (101–132) require an additional underground shuttle train after security.
- Avoid the central security area in T1 between 07:00–08:30; side lanes move quicker.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the official airport app and **confirm terminal by flight number** before every trip — the Asiana→T2 move broke the old alliance-based shortcut.
- If connecting T1↔T2 on one ticket, follow purple transfer signs to **connection security**, then the **shuttle train** (B1 level). Budget **35–45 min** minimum; experienced travelers allow **60+ min** in peaks.
- Capsule hotel on the transit side (between T1 and T2) lets you shower and nap without clearing immigration.
- Buy T-money card at AREX machines for seamless bus/metro rides into Seoul.
- Korean Air lounge in T2 (near gate 250) offers better food and showers than most paid options for eligible passengers.
- For early flights, sleep in the 24-hour rest zone on the 4th floor of T2 instead of fighting gate seating.
- Skip taxi queues at peak arrival times; walk one level up to the bus stop for faster departure.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T1 and T2 are **not walkable** — always use the internal shuttle train for transfers.
- T1 Concourse gates **101–132** require a separate underground shuttle train after security (~5 min intervals).
- Airside T1↔T2 transfer flow (official): connection security → shuttle train to Concourse (if needed) → shuttle train to other terminal → escalator up → gate. **Once you enter the transfer corridor you cannot go back.**
- Most international arrivals still process in T1 for non-T2 carriers; T2 handles Korean Air/Asiana/SkyTeam banks.
- Clear signage in English and Korean; follow color-coded lines for departures vs arrivals.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- Best lounge: Korean Air First/Business in T2—showers, quality Korean food, quiet zones.
- Solid paid option: The Lounge in T1 (near gate 40) for non-Korean Air passengers.
- Standout food: Korean BBQ and bibimbap counters on T2 departures (4F); avoid overpriced coffee chains.
- Quietest spot: T2 rest zone on the 4th floor near the transit hotel.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- AREX express is fastest and cheapest to Seoul Station (₩9,500, 43 min); runs every 15–30 min.
- KAL limousine buses are comfortable for specific hotel drops but slower in traffic.
- Taxis to central Seoul cost ₩60,000–80,000; use official stand to avoid overcharges.
- Short-term parking is expensive; long-term lots are cheaper but require shuttle.
