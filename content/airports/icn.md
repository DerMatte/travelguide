# ICN Airport Guide

> Fast reference for clearing ICN — the Asiana move to T2 broke the old Star Alliance = T1 shortcut.

## Quick Facts
- **Terminal 2 (from Jan 2026):** **Korean Air**, **Asiana Airlines** (moved **14 Jan 2026**), **Jin Air**, **Air Busan**, **Air Seoul**, plus **Delta, Air France, KLM**, and other **SkyTeam** partners. Check-in counters **G–J** on Departures Level 3.
- **Terminal 1:** Most other international carriers including **JAL, ANA, Air China, Jeju Air, T'way**, and many **Star Alliance / oneworld** airlines **except** the Korean Air group listed above.
- **Critical rule:** **Do not use alliance to guess your terminal** — Asiana is still Star Alliance but operates from **T2**. Always check your **e-ticket / airport app** by flight number.
- **Inter-terminal shuttle:** Free, every **5–10 min**; budget **12–15 min** including wait — terminals are **~2 km** apart with no practical walk.
- **MCT (same ticket):** **60 min** within the same terminal can work; **T1 ↔ T2** connections need **90 min+** including shuttle and possible re-screening.
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free and reliable; power outlets plentiful at gates.

## Security & Screening Tips
- **Full screening:** Liquids **100 ml**; laptops out unless CT lane signage says otherwise.
- **No PreCheck equivalent:** Arrive **2.5 h** early for international peaks.
- **T2 security:** Newer equipment — often faster than T1 when lines are equal.
- **Peak windows:** **06:00–09:00** and **18:00–22:00** — add **20–30 min** buffer.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **Asiana / Jin Air / Air Busan / Air Seoul → T2:** Since **Jan 2026**, these no longer use T1 — going to the wrong terminal costs **30–40 min** minimum.
- **T1 ↔ T2 connections:** Use the **free inter-terminal shuttle** from the **arrivals/landside** level — not a walk between terminals.
- **Transit hotel / capsules:** Between terminals on the transit side for shower/nap without clearing immigration — verify current rates.
- **T-money card:** Buy at **AREX** machines for seamless bus/metro in Seoul.
- **Avoid:** Assuming your **Star Alliance** flight is in **T1** — Korean Air group and Asiana are in **T2**.

## Terminals & Navigation
- **T1:** Larger variety of lounges and entertainment; slightly closer to Seoul on **AREX** (marginal).
- **T2:** Newer, single-building layout after security — no separate concourse train; **5th-floor observation deck** and outdoor gardens.
- **Shuttle only:** Walking between T1 and T2 is not practical.
- **Recommended buffers:**
  - Same terminal: **60–90 min**
  - T1 ↔ T2 on one ticket: **90 min** floor, **2 h** safer
  - Separate tickets: **3+ h**

## Lounges Food & Amenities
- **Korean Air First/Business (T2):** Best for eligible passengers — showers, Korean food, quiet zones near gate **250** area.
- **Paid option (T1):** **The Lounge** near gate **40** for non-Korean Air passengers in T1.
- **Food:** Korean BBQ and bibimbap counters on **T2** departures (**4F**) — airside chains are fine but overpriced.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **AREX express:** **Seoul Station** — **₩9,500**, **~43 min**; every **15–30 min**.
- **KAL limousine buses:** Hotel drops — slower in rush hour.
- **Taxi:** **₩60,000–80,000** to central Seoul — official stand only.
- **Parking:** Short-stay expensive; long-term lots require shuttle.
