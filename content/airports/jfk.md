# JFK Airport Guide

> New York’s global gateway — manageable on one ticket if you respect terminal hops and rebuild-related delays, unforgiving on separate tickets or tight international-to-domestic connections.

## Quick Facts
- **Active terminals (May 2026):** **1** (many international carriers), **4** (Delta hub + largest int'l mix), **5** (JetBlue), **7** (transitional — confirm with airline), **8** (American/oneworld hub). **Terminal 2** and **3** are demolished. **New Terminal 6** is opening in **phases from spring 2026** on the former T6/T7 site; **New Terminal One (NTO)** also begins phased opening mid-2026 — assignments will shift ([JFK airlines list](https://www.jfkairport.com/flights/airlines), [Terminal 6 timeline](https://www.portauthoritybuilds.com/redevelopment/us/en/jfk/planned-projects/terminal-6.html)).
- **MCT (practical, not airline marketing):**
  - **Same terminal, domestic → domestic:** **60–90 min** can work — still tight if inbound is late.
  - **Terminal change on one ticket:** AirTrain leg is **under 10 min** — but add **exit arrivals, walk to AirTrain, ride, re-check-in if needed, TSA** → budget **90 min–2 h**.
  - **International → US domestic:** CBP + baggage recheck + TSA — **2.5–3 h** floor; **3+ h** safer in peak.
  - **Separate tickets:** full arrival procedure — **3–4 h** minimum.
- **AirTrain:** **Free** between passenger terminals; **$8.75** one-way to **Jamaica** (LIRR/subway) or **Howard Beach** (A train); children **under 5** free; runs **24/7** every **5–15 min** ([JFK AirTrain](https://www.jfkairport.com/to-from-airport/air-train)).
- **Construction alert:** From **26 May 2026**, AirTrain service modifications affect the **Central Terminal Area** inner loop — expect longer waits and crowding; allow extra connection time.
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free airport Wi‑Fi — registration required; sessions may time out.
- **Liquids:** US **3-1-1** rule strictly enforced — quart-sized bag, **100 ml (3.4 oz)** containers.

## Security & Screening Tips
- **You re-clear TSA on terminal changes:** Even on one ticket, changing terminals means **new security at the departure terminal** — queue time dominates, not AirTrain time ([connections guide](https://www.jfkairport.com/flights/connections-guide)).
- **Peak pain windows:** Early morning (**06:00–09:00**) and evening (**16:00–20:00**) — Monday and holiday peaks are worst; add **20–30 min** buffer.
- **PreCheck and CLEAR:** Available in most active terminals — enrollment and lane hours vary; check TSA Wait Times on [jfkairport.com](https://www.jfkairport.com/) day of travel.
- **Terminal 4:** Largest terminal (~21M passengers/year) — multiple checkpoints; Delta publishes wait times in-app. **Int'l → domestic** flows through CBP then recheck — not a quick airside walk.
- **Terminal 5 (JetBlue):** Single-airline terminal — generally smoother for JetBlue-to-JetBlue, but int'l arrivals still clear CBP landside.
- **Terminal 8:** Renovated AA/oneworld hub — good for BA/AA connections **within T8**; terminal change still hurts.
- **International arrivals:** Everyone clears **CBP** on first entry to the U.S. Connecting passengers with through-checked bags still re-clear TSA at the departure terminal unless on a specific airline-arranged exception — don't assume otherwise.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **Verify operating terminal:** Codeshares lie — check the **operating carrier's** terminal (e.g. Finnair ticket on **American** → **Terminal 8**, not T1). Reconfirm **24 h** before travel during the 2026 rebuild.
- **AirTrain routing:** Follow signs from arrivals to **AirTrain** — all active terminals on the free loop. Trains every **5–15 min**; inner-loop construction may disrupt timing from **May 2026**.
- **Terminal 4 shuttle (airside, T4 only):** Delta runs a **free shuttle between T4 concourses** (stops near gates **A9, B23, B54**) — no extra TSA; useful for long T4 hikes, not for other terminals.
- **Int'l → domestic baggage:** You **must collect bags** at JFK even on one ticket, then **recheck** at the airline desk before TSA — this is where tight connections die.
- **Manhattan on a budget:** **AirTrain ($8.75) + E train** from Jamaica, or **A train** from Howard Beach — often faster than a taxi in rush hour with one manageable bag.
- **Food tip — Shake Shack:** Only in **T4** (Gates **B23** and **B37**) and **T8** (Gate **12**), both **airside**. You cannot reach them from other terminals without exiting and re-clearing security — order via app at peak meal times when you're already in the right terminal.
- **Terminal 6 transition (2026):** Airlines are moving from T7 to T6 in phases — double-check terminal on your boarding pass the week of travel; pickup zones and check-in counters change during migration.
- **Avoid:** Booking **60–75 min** connections that change terminals; assuming **Terminal 7** will still host your airline through 2026 (T6/T1 rebuild is moving carriers).

## Terminals & Navigation
- **Terminal 1:** Legacy international terminal — **Air France, Lufthansa, Korean Air, JAL**, and others. Being replaced by **New Terminal One** in phases; monitor airline notices.
- **Terminal 4:** **Delta** hub plus **Emirates, Virgin Atlantic, KLM, Air Canada**, and 30+ others — primary int'l arrivals facility with consolidated customs capacity.
- **Terminal 5:** **JetBlue** exclusively — int'l arrivals **05:00–22:30** at T5; some late int'l arrivals may use **T4** (verify on ticket).
- **Terminal 7:** **Transitional** — some carriers still here during rebuild; confirm before every trip.
- **Terminal 8:** **American Airlines** hub — **British Airways, Qatar, Iberia, Finnair** (oneworld) also operate here.
- **Terminal 6 (opening 2026):** New facility on former T6/T7 site — first gates expected **2026**, full build by **2028**; will connect to **T5**.
- **No airside shortcuts:** JFK terminals are **not** connected airside. Even T5↔T7 look close on a map — you still ride the **AirTrain** and pass through **TSA again**.
- **Transfer times (practical):**
  - AirTrain between adjacent terminals: **~5–10 min** ride + **5–10 min** walking/waiting
  - Full terminal change including TSA: **45–90 min** in normal conditions
  - Int'l → domestic with CBP: **2–3 h**
- **Recommended buffers:**
  - Same terminal: **60–90 min**
  - Terminal change, one ticket: **2 h** floor, **2.5 h** peaks
  - Int'l → US domestic: **3 h**
  - Separate tickets: **4 h**

## Lounges Food & Amenities
- **Terminal 4:** Widest lounge and dining selection — **Delta Sky Club**, **Virgin Atlantic Clubhouse**, **Emirates**, **SWISS**, and paid options. Best default for long int'l layovers **if you are already in T4**.
- **Terminal 5:** **JetBlue Mint** and **Roam** lounge — good for JetBlue connections; **T5 rooftop** (when open) for fresh air.
- **Terminal 8:** **American Admirals Club/Flagship** and **British Airways Galleries/Concorde Room** — worth it for oneworld connections **within T8**; Shake Shack near Gate 12 airside.
- **Terminal 1:** Lounge mix varies by carrier — quality depends on your airline; expect crowding during evening long-haul banks.
- **Food — reliable picks:** **Shake Shack (T4 Gates B23 & B37, T8 Gate 12)**, **Deep Blue Sushi (T5)**, **Lucy's Vietnamese (T4)** — order-ahead apps help at meal peaks. Generic grab-and-go is overpriced but predictable.
- **Overrated / traps:** Assuming any lounge is worth a terminal change with under **90 min** to board; skipping baggage recheck on int'l → domestic.
- **Power:** T5 gates **20–30** area and T4 gate clusters have USB outlets — charge after security, not at the gate during a delay.
- **Long layover:** **TWA Hotel** (connected to **T5** via AirTrain to **Terminal 5** stop) — iconic mid-century design, day rates available; **on-airport hotels** via AirTrain to **Federal Circle**.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **Best value to Manhattan:** **AirTrain ($8.75) + subway** — **E** from **Jamaica** or **A** from **Howard Beach**; **LIRR** from Jamaica is faster but pricier (~**30 min** to Penn Station off-peak).
- **Taxi / rideshare:** Flat **Manhattan taxi fare** from JFK (check current TLC rate — historically ~**$70+** with tolls/tip); Uber/Lyft surge heavily at peak. **Allow extra time** for roadway construction detours.
- **Between JFK and LGA/EWR:** No single-seat rail — bus combinations or rideshare; budget **60–90+ min** depending on traffic.
- **Parking:** On-airport lots fill at peak — pre-book if driving; **Lefferts Blvd** long-term with AirTrain link.
- **Pickup/drop-off lot:** **Lefferts Blvd AirTrain station** off-airport lot for meeting passengers without curbside chaos ([JFK alerts](https://www.jfkairport.com/)).
- **Rush hour reality:** **Van Wyck** and **Belt Parkway** clog **07:00–09:30** and **16:00–19:00** — subway via AirTrain often beats road to Midtown.
