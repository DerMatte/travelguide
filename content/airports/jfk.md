# JFK Airport Guide

> The most important things to know before you fly through New York’s busiest airport.

## Quick Facts
- **Terminals today:** **1** (many international), **4** (Delta + international), **5** (JetBlue), **7** (legacy BA/Star — slated for replacement), **8** (American/oneworld). **Terminal 6** is opening in **phases from 2026** on the north side (adjacent to T5); **Terminal 7** will eventually close after airlines move. **New Terminal One** (south side mega-terminal) is a separate multi-year project — do not confuse with T6.
- **MCT (same ticket, practical):**
  - **Same terminal:** **60–75 min** floor.
  - **Terminal change (e.g. T4 → T8):** **90 min** airline minimum is tight — budget **2 h**, **2.5 h** in peaks.
  - **Separate tickets:** Immigration, bags, terminal transfer, check-in — **4 hours minimum** (BA-style international self-transfer guidance).
- **AirTrain:** **Free** between airport terminals; **$8.75** when you enter/exit at **Jamaica** (LIRR/subway) or **Howard Beach** (A subway). Children under **5** ride free.
- **Liquids:** TSA **3-1-1** — quart bag, **3.4 oz (100 ml)** per container unless a checkpoint posts CT-lane exceptions.

## Security & Screening Tips
- **Multiple checkpoints per terminal** — use your airline app for the correct side (T4 has several zones).
- **PreCheck and CLEAR** are available in most terminals and usually worth it for frequent flyers.
- **Peak queues:** Late afternoon (**15:00–19:00**) and Sunday evenings are consistently slow; international morning banks (**08:00–11:00**) also spike.
- **T4** is the highest-volume terminal — allow extra time even with PreCheck during Delta long-haul banks.
- **Construction (from May 2026):** Port Authority warns of **AirTrain inner-loop modifications** affecting terminals **1, 4, 5, 7, 8** — longer waits and crowding possible; pad connection time.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **Terminal change = AirTrain:** After clearing any required immigration/customs, use **AirTrain** between terminals — typically **under 10 min** train time plus walking (**5–15 min** station-to-gate).
- **T5 ↔ future T6:** JetBlue’s north campus will link T5 and new **T6** — watch airline notices if your Lufthansa/Star flight moves from **T1/T7** to **T6** in 2026–2027.
- **Delta Sky Club (T4):** Often less crowded than the flagship clubs at peak — good for domestic connections; international Wing may differ by concourse.
- **Shake Shack:** **T4** (near gates **B23/B37**) and **T8** (near **Gate 12**) — order on the app to skip lines.
- **Charging + seating:** **T5** has reliable outlets near gates **20–30**; quieter mezzanine seating exists upstairs in several terminals — grab power right after security.
- **Manhattan hack:** **LIRR from Jamaica** + AirTrain is often faster than a taxi in **midtown rush hour**; **A train from Howard Beach** is cheaper but slower. Budget **60–90 min** door-to-door to Penn/Grand Central in peaks.
- **Avoid:** Booking **60 min** domestic connections between **T4 and T8** on separate tickets — immigration and AirTrain alone can consume that.

## Terminals & Navigation
- **Layout:** Terminals ring the **Central Terminal Area**; **AirTrain** is the realistic connector landside (no single continuous airside walkway between all terminals).
- **Walking times (landside + AirTrain, no queues):**
  - Adjacent terminal pair via AirTrain: **15–25 min** total
  - T4 ↔ T8: **20–35 min**
  - T1 ↔ T8: **25–40 min**
- **2026–2028 transition:** Airlines shifting to **T6** and eventual **T7 demolition** — **reconfirm terminal on your ticket** within 72 hours of departure.
- **Common mistakes:** Assuming JFK is one building; forgetting **$8.75 AirTrain** surcharge on top of subway/LIRR fare; clearing US immigration on arrival then expecting a **20 min** connection.

## Lounges Food & Amenities
- **Delta Sky Club / One lounges (T4):** Default for SkyTeam premium — quality varies by concourse; T4 is large — confirm gate vs. lounge location.
- **JetBlue Mint / lounges (T5):** Strong product for Mint; fewer alliance walk-in options.
- **American Admirals / Flagship (T8):** Best for oneworld AA itineraries; Flagship Dining by invitation/cabin rules.
- **Star/oneworld (T1/T7 until moves):** **Lufthansa**, **SWISS**, **Cathay** etc. — lounge placement will shift with **T6** rollout; check airline site the week of travel.
- **Pay-for:** **Priority Pass** locations exist in **T4/T7** (participation changes) — backup when status does not apply.
- **Food:** **Shake Shack (T4/T8)**; **T5** marketplace options; **T4** has the broadest sit-down selection. Avoid full-service restaurants with under **60 min** to board.
- **Water:** Refill fountains post-security in newer concourses; bottled water is expensive — fill before long hauls.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **AirTrain + subway:** **E** or **A** from **Jamaica/Howard Beach** — total fare roughly **$11.75** (subway **$3** + AirTrain **$8.75**) with OMNY/contactless; unlimited MetroCards **do not** cover AirTrain.
- **LIRR:** **Jamaica** to Penn Station **~$5.25–$7.25** off-peak/peak plus **$8.75** AirTrain — fastest to midtown when timed well.
- **Taxi / ride-hail:** Flat-rate zones exist to Manhattan; surge pricing applies — can win late night vs. waiting for rail.
- **Uber/Lyft:** Designated pickup areas per terminal — follow app pin; construction may shift zones.
- **Parking:** Terminal garages are costly — consider off-airport or LIRR if staying in NYC proper.
- **Construction tip:** From **May 26, 2026**, expect **AirTrain delays** on the terminal loop — leave extra time for **all** ground connections.
