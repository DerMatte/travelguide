# LAS Airport Guide

> Fast, practical guide to moving through Harry Reid without wasting time on lines or bad connections.

## Quick Facts
- Two terminals (T1 and T3) with no airside connection
- Southwest is largest carrier, concentrated in T1
- Free tram runs continuously between terminals
- Heavy 24/7 traffic with slot machines throughout
- 15-25 minutes to Las Vegas Strip
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free airport Wi‑Fi — select network and accept terms ([Harry Reid Wi‑Fi](https://www.harryreidairport.com/AirportServices/WiFi)).
- **Power:** Outlets at T3 gates and T1 yoga room; grab one before peak banks.
- **Water:** Refill fountains post-security in T3; T1 has fewer — buy landside or bring empty bottle.

## Security & Screening Tips
- T1 security backs up badly 5-9 AM and 3-8 PM; use CLEAR or PreCheck when available.
- T3 security is consistently faster; switch terminals early if your airline permits.
- Avoid the main T1 checkpoint if flying out of Concourse D or E—use the side lanes.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the Harry Reid app for live tram and gate updates.
- If connecting T1-T3, ride the tram instead of walking the long outdoor path.
- Late-night arrivals: taxis have shorter waits than rideshares during surge.
- Grab food in T3 even on T1 departures—the tram ride is faster than T1 lines.
- Use the free cell phone lot for pickups; it’s well marked and rarely full.
- Check bags at Southwest counters early; they move faster than the app suggests.
- Quietest seating is on the upper mezzanine near T3 gates or the yoga room in T1.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T1 handles most domestic flights across five concourses; T3 serves international and select domestic.
- Free tram is the only practical link—runs every 4-7 minutes.
- Walking between T1 concourses takes 10-15 minutes; budget extra time for connections.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- Centurion Lounge in T3 is the best paid option if you have access.
- Standout quick food: Shake Shack (T3) and In-N-Out (T1 landside).
- Avoid the central T1 food court—smaller gate-side spots have shorter lines.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Rideshare pickup on level 2 moves fastest outside peak arrival banks.
- Short-term parking is expensive; long-term lots are cheaper with free shuttles.
- Taxis to the Strip are reliable and usually faster than rideshares after midnight.
