# LAS Airport Guide

> Practical, no-fluff guide to moving through Harry Reid International Airport quickly and with minimal stress.

## Quick Facts
- Two terminals (T1 and T3) with no airside connection; re-clear security if switching.
- Southwest dominates; Spirit, Frontier, Delta, American, and United also operate significant schedules.
- TSA PreCheck lanes at both terminals; CLEAR available at T1 and T3.
- Free tram runs every 4-7 minutes between terminals.
- Security is usually fast but backs up after 4pm on Fridays and weekends.
- Slot machines and gaming areas are everywhere; they do not affect security flow.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Use the TSA app or airport website for current wait times; T1 A/B checkpoints are often fastest.
- PreCheck and CLEAR lines move quickly except during 4-7pm departure banks on weekends.
- Avoid the main Terminal 1 checkpoint between 3:30-6:30pm if possible; use the D-gate checkpoint instead when flying Southwest.
- Liquids in clear bags are strictly enforced; no exceptions at this airport.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the Harry Reid app for real-time tram and security updates before you arrive.
- If connecting from an E-gate flight to a Southwest flight, exit security, take the tram, and re-clear at the D checkpoint to save time.
- Fill water bottles at the post-security fillers near gates A14 and B22; Terminal 3 has only one reliable station.
- Park at the Terminal 3 garage for easier access if flying Delta or international arrivals; it is rarely full.
- Use the outdoor walkway between terminals only if the tram is down; it is exposed and longer than it looks.
- Southwest flyers can check bags at the curb and proceed directly to the A/B checkpoint to skip the main line.
- Avoid the central food court between 11am-1pm and 5-7pm; head to gate-area locations instead.
- If your flight is delayed, the T1 A/B checkpoint usually has shorter re-entry lines than the main checkpoint.

## Terminals & Navigation
- Terminal 1 handles most flights; Terminal 3 is used by Delta, LATAM, and some international carriers.
- Tram ride between terminals takes 4 minutes; walking takes 12-15 minutes.
- No moving walkways on the tram route; budget extra time with luggage.
- Gates A/B are closest to the main T1 checkpoint; C and D require additional walking or people-mover.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- The Centurion Lounge in Terminal 3 is the best paid option; access rules are standard Amex policy.
- Shake Shack and La Tapenade in Terminal 1 are consistently good; everything else is average.
- Quietest seating is near gate A19 and the far end of Terminal 3 E gates.
- No standout free lounges; paid day passes are rarely worth it unless you have long delays.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Rideshare pickup is on level 2 of the parking garages; follow signs to avoid curb confusion.
- Rental cars are in a separate terminal reached by free shuttle; allow 15 minutes after baggage claim.
- Short-term parking in the T1 garage is convenient for quick drop-offs but fills on weekend evenings.
- The 108 bus to the Strip is cheap but slow; rideshare is faster for most travelers.
