# LAX Airport Guide

> Practical, no-fluff information with an emphasis on actually useful tips and tricks.

## Quick Facts
- **Layout:** U-shaped complex — **Terminals 1–8** plus **Tom Bradley International Terminal (TBIT)**. Since the **2023 T3–TBIT airside connector**, every terminal is reachable **without leaving security** (walk can be **2+ miles** end-to-end).
- **MCT (same ticket, practical):**
  - **Same terminal / adjacent pier:** **45–60 min** floor; **75 min** comfortable.
  - **TBIT ↔ T4/T5 or cross-campus (e.g. T1 → T7):** **75–90 min** floor; **2 h** in peaks — walking adds up fast.
  - **Separate tickets:** immigration, bags, check-in, security — budget **3+ hours** minimum for domestic self-transfer; **4+ hours** if international arrival.
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free airport Wi‑Fi in terminals (registration may be required — check flylax.com).
- **Liquids:** TSA **3-1-1** — containers **3.4 oz (100 ml)** max in one quart-sized bag; CT lanes at some checkpoints may allow laptops/liquids to stay in the bag — follow posted signs.

## Security & Screening Tips
- **Peak pain:** Early morning (**05:00–09:00**) and late afternoon (**16:00–20:00**) — add **20–30 min** beyond airline advice.
- **PreCheck** lanes are usually much faster where available; **CLEAR** can help but does not eliminate queues at busy banks.
- **Terminal 4, TBIT, and renovated T3** tend to have newer checkpoint layouts; **T1** can still feel cramped during Southwest peaks.
- **International arrivals:** You must clear **CBP**, claim bags (unless through-checked on one ticket with airside transfer rules), and may need to **re-check** before domestic connections — not a true “stay airside” connection for most itineraries.
- **Regional Terminal (American Eagle):** Separate from T4/T5 — **airside shuttle** required; add **15–20 min** to connection math.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **Airside connector map:** TBIT sits between **T3 and T4** — international-to-American connections are walkable post-security; north side (**T1–T3**) and south side (**T4–T8**) link through TBIT or underground walkways (T4–T8).
- **In-N-Out is not in the terminal:** The famous **9149 S Sepulveda Blvd** location is **~1 mile landside** — you must **exit security** and allow time to return. **Shake Shack (T3, airside near Gate 34)** is the realistic burger fix inside the secure area.
- **Shake Shack / Chick-fil-A:** Order via app during peak meal times — lines at T3 Shake Shack spike when Delta banks depart.
- **TBIT lounges & food:** Best concentration of **oneworld/Star** lounges and sit-down dining — worth routing there if your gate allows without missing a south-side departure.
- **Cell phone lot:** Free waiting for pickups — useful for drivers; does not replace **LAX-it** for standard Uber/Lyft.
- **Rideshare hack:** **Uber Black / Lyft Lux** and private cars can still pick up **at terminal curbs**; standard Uber/Lyft use **LAX-it** — walking to T1-adjacent LAX-it can beat a packed shuttle during surges.
- **Power:** TBIT mezzanine and gate areas near **150+** have strong charging; grab outlets right after security, not during a delay at the gate.
- **Baggage claim:** International carousels often load **far end first** — position accordingly.

## Terminals & Navigation
- **Airside walking times (order-of-magnitude, no queues):**
  - Adjacent terminal hop (e.g. TBIT ↔ T4): **5–8 min**
  - T3 ↔ T7 via connectors: **15–25 min**
  - T1 ↔ T8 full U-walk: **40–60+ min** — treat as a last resort for tight connections
- **Airline homes (check your ticket — moves happen):**
  - **T1:** Southwest and others
  - **T2/T3:** Delta (check-in concentrated in **T3** after terminal moves)
  - **TBIT:** Most wide-body international
  - **T4/T5:** American
  - **T6–T8:** Alaska, United, and partners (verify on flylax.com)
- **Common mistakes:** Assuming “connected airside” means **short** when gates are opposite ends; missing **Regional Terminal** shuttle for Eagle flights; booking **In-N-Out** with under **90 min** layover landside.

## Lounges Food & Amenities
- **Delta Sky Club (T3):** Primary SkyTeam lounge — solid for West Coast hops; crowded during morning banks.
- **TBIT:** **Star Alliance** (e.g. Star Lounge) and **oneworld** lounges — best bet for long-haul premium; verify alliance and terminal on boarding pass.
- **American Admirals / Flagship (T4):** Convenient for AA connections; Flagship worth it only in qualifying cabins.
- **United Clubs (T7/T8):** Useful for Star connections on the south arm.
- **Pay-for:** Limited independent day-pass options vs. European hubs — check Priority Pass app for current TBIT/T4 participants.
- **Food — actually good (airside):** **Shake Shack (T3)**; **Chick-fil-A (T1)**; **TBIT** sit-down options beat generic newsstand food. **In-N-Out** only landside (see above).
- **Water:** Bring an empty bottle — refill stations vary by terminal; bottled water is full airport markup.
- **Avoid:** Long sit-down meals in TBIT when departing from **T7/T8** with under **90 min** to board.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **FlyAway bus:** Nonstop **24/7** to **Union Station** and **Van Nuys** — **$12.75** one-way (contactless or app; **no cash**). Pickup at **Lower/Arrivals** blue columns at each terminal; allow **40–75 min** to downtown depending on traffic.
- **LAX-it:** Standard **Uber/Lyft/taxi** pickups — free green **LAX-it shuttle** from arrivals level (~**7 min** frequency) or **~8 min walk** from T1. Premium/black car and private vehicles still use **terminal curbs**.
- **Metro:** **K Line** (Crenshaw) connects to LAX via **LAX Metro Transit Center** — free shuttle from terminals; check current rail map before relying on it for downtown (may require transfers).
- **Taxi:** Available at LAX-it; limited curbside taxis at **T7** and **Parking Structure 3 (TBIT/T3 area)** per airport guidance.
- **Parking:** Central terminals use structure/garage parking — book ahead for multi-day; economy lots farther out with shuttle.
- **Rush hour:** **405/105** corridors clog **07:00–10:00** and **15:00–19:00** — FlyAway or rail often beats driving to central LA.
