# LGW Airport Guide

> LGW splits traffic across two terminals with no airside link, so terminal awareness and timing are the only variables that matter.

## Quick Facts
- North Terminal handles most long-haul; South Terminal is easyJet-heavy.
- Free inter-terminal shuttle runs every 3-5 minutes, 24/7.
- **MCT (separate tickets / terminal change):** no airside link — exit, shuttle, re-clear security; budget **2 h+** minimum between North and South.
- No TSA PreCheck equivalent; standard security only.
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free **Gatwick FREE WiFi** — email registration; sessions expire ([Gatwick Wi‑Fi](https://www.gatwickairport.com/at-the-airport/wifi)).
- **Power:** Outlets at some gate-area seating; scarce at peak — charge in No.1 Lounge or landside mezzanine if you have time.
- Gatwick Express, Thameslink and Southern all serve the same station.
- Drop-off charge £5 for private cars; free for 10 min if using official pick-up zones.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Download the Gatwick app and check live wait times before you leave home.
- South Terminal security is usually faster before 07:00 and after 20:00.
- Liquids in 100 ml containers only; no trusted-traveler lane exists.
- Families and passengers with reduced mobility can use the fast-track lane for free if they ask at the entrance.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Book the free North Terminal short-stay car park and walk to the terminal if your flight is from South; the shuttle is often quicker than circling for South spaces.
- Use the free shower in the North Terminal arrivals (level 1) after a red-eye; it’s rarely busy.
- If you have a long layover, take the free shuttle to the opposite terminal for better food and seating, then return.
- Thameslink trains to central London are £3-4 cheaper than Gatwick Express and only 8 minutes slower.
- The Premier Inn next to the station offers day rooms from £49 if you need a nap between flights.
- Avoid the South Terminal WHSmith at peak times; the North Terminal branch has shorter queues.
- Keep your boarding pass on your phone; paper copies are rarely requested after security.

## Terminals & Navigation
- Both terminals sit above the same railway station; follow signs immediately after exiting the train.
- Walking time between terminals via the shuttle is 10-12 minutes including wait.
- Once airside, you cannot switch terminals without exiting and re-clearing security.
- Buses to the car-rental centre leave from the lower forecourt of each terminal every 10 minutes.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- The No.1 Lounge in North Terminal (airside) offers the best hot food and quiet seating for the £35-45 day rate.
- Comptoir Libanais in North Terminal serves reliable mezze and has power sockets at most tables.
- The Grove in South Terminal is the only decent sit-down option after security when North is unreachable.
- Free water fountains are located just past security in both terminals; bring a bottle.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Gatwick Express is fastest to Victoria but costs £22+; Thameslink is cheaper and reaches more stations.
- Official pick-up zone is free for 10 minutes; after that it’s £5 for 30 minutes.
- Short-stay parking is £4.50 per hour; long-stay is £9-12 per day but requires the free shuttle.
- National Express coaches to Heathrow take 45-60 minutes and run every 15-30 minutes.
