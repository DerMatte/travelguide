# LIM Airport Guide

> One-stop reference for navigating Lima's single terminal efficiently and avoiding the worst queues.

## Quick Facts
- Single terminal with domestic (A-B) and international (C-D) piers connected airside.
- LATAM, Sky Airline, and Avianca dominate; major U.S. carriers use Terminal C gates.
- New 2024 terminal features faster immigration with e-gates for many nationalities.
- Free Wi-Fi works but slows during evening peaks; download boarding passes offline.
- No airside hotel; nearest sleep options require exiting and re-clearing security.
- Currency exchange rates inside are poor—use ATMs before security.

## Security & Screening Tips
- International departures require 3 hours; domestic 90 minutes minimum.
- Liquids follow standard 100 ml rules; no PreCheck equivalent.
- Mornings (05:00-09:00) and evenings (18:00-22:00) create the longest lines—use the far-left lanes for families or the rightmost for business class when open.
- Shoes must come off; laptops stay in bags at most lanes.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Clear immigration first, then eat at Punto Azul before security if your flight is after 10:00.
- Download the LIM app for gate push notifications; terminal screens update slowly.
- For early flights, check in online and head straight to the priority security lane on the far right.
- Need soles? Withdraw at the BBVA ATM near arrivals before taking a taxi.
- Moving walkways on Level 2 cut the long walk to gates D30+ in half.
- If connecting from domestic to international, stay airside—no need to recheck bags.
- Quiet seating with charging ports is found past gate C12 near the windows.

## Terminals & Navigation
- One building: domestic left, international right; airside corridor links both.
- Walking time from check-in to farthest gate averages 12-15 minutes.
- Follow yellow signage for international departures; blue for domestic.
- Common mistake: exiting security for food—re-entry lines are long.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- No standout paid lounges; the LATAM lounge is average and often crowded.
- Punto Azul (pre-security) serves the best fresh ceviche and causa.
- Post-security grab-and-go: Starbucks and a small Subway; avoid the overpriced burger spot.
- Free water fountains located near gates C8 and D22.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Official taxi desk in arrivals offers fixed rates to Miraflores (~S/50, 45 min).
- Uber and Beat work but require walking to the departures curb.
- Short-term parking is expensive; long-term lots fill by 08:00 on weekdays.
- Metropolitano bus to downtown requires a 10-minute walk to the station.
