# LIS Airport Guide

> Fast, no-nonsense guide to clearing Lisbon's single busy terminal and reaching the city quickly.

## Quick Facts
- One primary terminal (T1) for almost all flights; T2 serves only Ryanair and a few others.
- TAP Air Portugal operates the majority of long-haul and Schengen connections.
- Red-line metro to downtown in 20 minutes (€1.65); Aerobus is slower and more expensive.
- Security and immigration are combined; no separate PreCheck equivalent.
- Free unlimited Wi-Fi; limited power outlets near most gates.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Arrive at least 2.5 hours before departure; lines regularly hit 40 minutes during morning and evening peaks.
- Keep liquids and electronics in a single tray—staff are strict but efficient.
- Families and passengers with reduced mobility get a shorter dedicated lane; use it if eligible.
- Avoid the central security area 6-9am and 4-7pm; try the side lanes near check-in rows 30-40.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Walk the covered 12-minute path to T2 instead of waiting for the shuttle when transferring.
- Charge devices at the quiet seating area behind Pastaria after security.
- Buy the Viva Viagem metro card at the airport station for seamless city travel including trams.
- Gate changes are common—set an alarm for 45 minutes before departure and re-check the app.
- The ANA app pushes gate and delay alerts faster than the airport screens.
- If your flight is delayed, the mezzanine level above check-in has free seating and fewer people.
- TAP passengers can use the lounge even on short connections if flying business or Star Alliance Gold.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T1 is a single long building; Schengen gates A, non-Schengen gates B-C.
- Minimum connection time posted at 45 minutes for Schengen-to-Schengen; allow 75 minutes otherwise.
- Moving walkways run the full length of the terminal; walking end-to-end takes 12-15 minutes.
- T2 is only reachable by shuttle or the 12-minute walk—do not rely on the bus during rush.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- TAP Lounge (paid access available) offers decent hot food and showers; better than the paid alternatives.
- Pastaria (post-security) serves fresh pasta and has the quietest seating with power outlets.
- The central food court is loud and overpriced; head to the far end near gate 46 for calmer options.
- Free showers are available in the TAP Lounge only; no public paid showers.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Metro red line is fastest and cheapest to the center; buy a reloadable Viva Viagem card.
- Official taxis have a fixed €20-25 rate to the city; avoid unofficial drivers outside arrivals.
- Short-term parking (P1) is €2.50 per hour; long-term (P6) is cheaper but requires a shuttle.
- Uber and Bolt pick-up is on level -1; follow signs to avoid the taxi touts.
