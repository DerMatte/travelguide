# MAD Airport Guide

> Actionable, up-to-date guide to moving through Madrid-Barajas with minimal stress and wasted time.

## Quick Facts
- Four terminals; T4/T4S manage the majority of long-haul and Iberia/oneworld flights.
- Primary hub for Iberia and oneworld alliance carriers.
- Metro Line 8, Renfe C1/C10 trains, and express buses reach central Madrid in 30-45 minutes.
- Security lines are longest 6-9am and 5-8pm; T4 consistently busiest.
- Free Wi-Fi and reliable mobile coverage available in all terminals.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Use the AENA app for live security wait times before heading to the checkpoint.
- Priority/fast-track lanes exist for eligible passengers; arrive early if you lack access.
- Liquids and electronics rules follow standard EU limits; 100 ml containers in a single clear bag.
- Avoid peak windows when possible; T4S often moves faster than main T4 for non-Schengen flights.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Landside food in T4 departures is markedly better and cheaper than airside; eat before clearing security.
- The free automated people mover between T4 and T4S runs every 4 minutes; no need to rush.
- If connecting from T1-T3 to T4, take the free inter-terminal bus rather than walking the long corridors.
- Renfe train tickets to central Madrid can be bought at machines with contactless cards; skip the ticket office queue.
- Quiet seating areas exist near gates 70-80 in T4S after the main crowds thin.
- Download the AENA app for gate changes and real-time queue data instead of relying on screens.
- For early flights, the T4 metro entrance is less crowded than the main departures level.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T4 and T4S are linked by a 5-minute underground automated train; allow 10-15 minutes total between them.
- T1-T3 are clustered together; T4 sits separately and requires the bus or metro for transfers.
- Walking times within T4 are long; use moving walkways and plan extra time for connections over 90 minutes.
- Common mistake: assuming all terminals are walkable; always confirm your terminal on the boarding pass.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- Iberia Velázquez and Velázquez Premium lounges in T4 offer solid food and showers for eligible passengers.
- Landside T4 has better Spanish ham and coffee options than most post-security outlets.
- Quiet zones with power outlets are located past security near gates 80-90 in T4S.
- Avoid overpriced airside chains; the best value remains before security.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Renfe Cercanías C1 or C10 trains are fastest and most reliable to central Madrid; buy tickets at machines.
- Metro Line 8 works well but requires a change at Nuevos Ministerios for most city-center stops.
- Airport express bus (24-hour service) is a solid backup when trains are delayed.
- Short-term parking is expensive; long-term lots require a free shuttle and are cheaper for multi-day stays.
