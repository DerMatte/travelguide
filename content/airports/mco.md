# MCO Airport Guide

> Orlando International Airport rewards early arrival and simple navigation choices over complex hacks.

## Quick Facts
- Terminals A and B are the only terminals; all gates reached on foot via moving walkways.
- Southwest, JetBlue, and Spirit dominate; Delta and United use Terminal B primarily.
- Rental cars, rideshares, and taxis all depart from the same ground level after the tram ride.
- No airside connections between terminals; re-clear security if switching sides.
- Heavy morning and evening leisure surges tied to theme-park flight banks.

## Security & Screening Tips
- TSA PreCheck lanes exist in both terminals; CLEAR is also available but lines can still form.
- Avoid 6-10am and 4-8pm when possible; those windows routinely exceed 30-45 minute waits.
- Families with young children and large strollers create bottlenecks at the standard lanes.
- Check the airport website or app for real-time lane status before heading to security.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Exit the tram one level early and walk to rideshare pickup on the arrivals roadway to skip the main queue.
- Fill reusable bottles at the free stations located just past security in both terminals.
- Use the moving walkways between A and B rather than waiting for the internal tram during moderate crowds.
- If your flight is in Terminal B, consider entering through Terminal A when A security is visibly shorter.
- Download the official Orlando Airports app for tram status and parking garage counts before landing.
- Pre-book off-site parking only if you are staying more than five days; on-site garages are rarely full.
- Carry a printed or saved boarding pass; cell service drops in the tram tunnel and some gate areas.

## Terminals & Navigation
- Walk between Terminal A and Terminal B using the clearly marked moving walkways; typical transit time is 8-12 minutes.
- The rental car tram departs from Level 3 in both terminals and runs every 2-3 minutes.
- Common mistake: following signs for “Terminal Link” when you actually need the moving walkway to change terminals airside.
- Gates are numbered sequentially within each terminal; no need to memorize complex layouts.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- The Delta Sky Club in Terminal B is the only major alliance lounge; access rules are standard and posted.
- Terminal A has more varied quick-service options near gates A1-A10; Terminal B food clusters are smaller.
- Quiet seating areas exist near gates A21-A29 and B22-B29 outside peak departure banks.
- Water bottle refill stations and charging tables are reliable in both terminals post-security.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Rideshare pickup is on the arrivals level (Level 1) outside both terminals; follow signs to avoid taxi stands.
- On-site garages are convenient but confirm space on the airport app during holiday periods.
- The rental car tram is reliable except during rare maintenance windows; have a backup plan for late arrivals.
- Check before travel for any temporary construction affecting the arrivals roadway.
