# MIA Airport Guide

> Practical, no-fluff guide to moving through Miami International Airport fast and with minimal stress.

## Quick Facts
- **Layout:** One horseshoe-shaped terminal with concourses **D, E, F, G, H, J** — check your **concourse letter**, not a terminal number.
- **North Terminal (Concourse D):** **American Airlines** hub — largest concourse (~51 gates).
- **Central Terminal (Concourses E–G):** Mix of domestic and international carriers.
- **South Terminal (Concourses H–J):** **Delta, United, Southwest**, JetBlue, Spirit, Frontier, and many intercontinental carriers.
- **Airside connections are limited:** Only **D↔E** (bridge near **D31/E2**) and **H↔J** (internal walkway) stay post-security. **D/E/F/G do not connect airside to H/J** — you must exit and re-clear TSA.
- **MIA Mover:** Free automated train on **Level 3** to the **rental car center** and **MiamiCentral** (Metrorail, Tri-Rail, Brightline) — **not** an airside concourse shuttle.
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free airport Wi‑Fi — browser login required; sessions may expire.

## Security & Screening Tips
- **Re-screening is common:** Connecting **North/Central → South** (e.g. **D → J**) means **exit security, walk landside on Level 3, new TSA line** — budget **60–90 min**, not a quick walk.
- **Peak pain windows:** Early morning (**05:00–08:00**) and late afternoon (**15:00–19:00**); add **20–30 min** buffer beyond airline MCT.
- **Use the MIA app** or airport website for real-time checkpoint wait times.
- **CLEAR + PreCheck:** Available at major checkpoints — still subject to TSA screening.
- **Concourse-specific checkpoints:** When flying **H/J**, enter through the **South Terminal** security — don't clear at **D** expecting to walk airside to **J**.
- **International arrivals:** Everyone clears **CBP** on first U.S. entry; connecting passengers with through-checked bags still **recheck and re-clear TSA** unless on a specific airline exception — don't assume otherwise.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **Verify concourse first:** Your boarding pass letter (**D** vs **J**) determines which security checkpoint and whether you can stay airside.
- **D → J connection:** Walk **landside Level 3** between North and South terminals (~**15–20 min**), then **new TSA** at South — the **MIA Mover does not help** for this; it only serves rental cars and MiamiCentral.
- **D ↔ E shortcut:** If both gates are in **D and E**, use the **airside bridge near D31/E2** — no extra TSA.
- **Skytrain (Concourse D):** Above Concourse D only — moves you along the **D** pier, not to other concourses.
- **Quiet seating:** Upper level near **Gate J17** (South) or past **Gate H** moving walkway when you're already in the right concourse.
- **Avoid:** Assuming "same airport" means one secure zone — **American (D) ↔ Delta (J)** is a landside hike plus full re-screening.

## Terminals & Navigation
- **North Terminal:** Concourse **D** only (American).
- **Central Terminal:** Concourses **E, F, G** — **E↔F↔G** are walkable airside within the central complex.
- **South Terminal:** Concourses **H, J** — connected airside to each other; **not** to North/Central.
- **Walking times (landside Level 3, between terminal sections):** North ↔ South **15–25 min** depending on gate.
- **Recommended connection buffers (practical):**
  - Same concourse or linked airside pair (**D↔E**, **H↔J**): **60–90 min**
  - North/Central ↔ South (e.g. **D → J**): **2 h** floor, **2.5 h** in peaks
  - International → domestic with CBP: **3 h**
  - Separate tickets: **4 h**

## Lounges Food & Amenities
- **American Admirals Club (Concourse D)** and **Centurion Lounge (near E)** — best paid options if you're already in the right concourse.
- **Standout food:** **La Carreta** (Cuban, main terminal landside) and **La Sandwicherie** (Concourse D airside).
- **Overrated / traps:** Trekking to a **D** lounge when departing from **J** with under **90 min** — you'll miss the flight.
- **Power:** Gate areas vary — charge right after security, not during a delay at the gate.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **Tri-Rail / Metrorail:** **MIA Mover** (free, Level 3) to **MiamiCentral** — Tri-Rail to downtown/Fort Lauderdale; Metrorail to Miami (~**25 min** to Government Center).
- **Rental cars:** Same **MIA Mover** to the consolidated rental center — avoid the curb during cruise turnarounds (**14:00–18:00**).
- **Uber/Lyft:** Pickup on arrivals level; surge common **16:00–19:00**.
- **Parking:** Short-term garage above check-in; long-term lots require shuttle.
