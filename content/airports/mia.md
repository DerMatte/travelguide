# MIA Airport Guide

> Practical, no-fluff guide to moving through Miami International Airport fast — concourse changes are the trap, not the train.

## Quick Facts
- **Layout:** One terminal building with concourses **D** (North / American hub), **E, F, G** (Central), and **H, J** (South). Not all concourses connect airside.
- **Airside connections (limited):** **D ↔ E** and **H ↔ J** only. Moving between **D/E** and **F/G/H/J** (or **F/G** and **D**) requires **exiting security and re-clearing TSA** at the destination concourse.
- **MIA Mover:** Free automated train on **Level 3** between the **main terminal** and the **Rental Car Center / Miami Central Station** (Metrorail, Tri-Rail, buses) — **not** an inter-concourse shuttle.
- **Skytrain:** Runs **inside Concourse D only** — end-to-end on the North Terminal pier.
- **Peak periods:** Early morning Europe departures and evening Latin America arrivals; summer afternoon thunderstorms cause delays **Jun–Sep**.

## Security & Screening Tips
- **Concourse-specific checkpoints:** Use the checkpoint closest to your concourse — walking airside between distant concourses is often impossible.
- **MIA app / website:** Real-time checkpoint wait times when available.
- **CLEAR + PreCheck:** Main checkpoints; often under **10 min** off-peak.
- **Avoid central checkpoint peaks:** **06:00–08:00** and **16:00–18:00** — use **E** or **J** checkpoints when your gate is there.
- **Liquids:** Standard US **3-1-1** rule strictly enforced.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **Check both concourses on your boarding pass:** A **D → J** connection is **not** a short walk — budget **exit security, walk landside, new TSA** → **45–90 min** in normal conditions.
- **D ↔ E airside:** The only easy North–Central airside link — useful for American connections between these piers.
- **H ↔ J airside:** South Terminal link — don't assume you can reach **D** from here without re-screening.
- **MIA Mover for ground transport:** Use it for **rental cars, Metrorail, Tri-Rail** — not for gate-to-gate connections.
- **American remote bag drop:** Arrivals-level remote drop when main counters are slammed — verify it's open for your flight.
- **Avoid:** Booking a **60 min** connection that changes concourses across the **D/F divide** — one of MIA's most common misconnect patterns.

## Terminals & Navigation
- **North Terminal (Concourse D):** American Airlines hub; **Skytrain** along the pier; international arrivals facility serves **D, E, F**.
- **Central Terminal (E, F, G):** **E** has a satellite pier (train between main and satellite); **F/G** are walkable from Central checkpoints.
- **South Terminal (H, J):** **H ↔ J** connected airside; good MIA Mover access on Level 3 for ground transport.
- **Walking times (within one concourse):** **3–10 min** gate-to-gate; **cross-concourse landside + TSA:** **45–90 min**.
- **International connections:** Clear **CBP** on first US entry; recheck bags and re-clear TSA for domestic connections unless on a specific airline-arranged exception.

## Lounges Food & Amenities
- **American Admirals Club (Concourse D)** and **Centurion Lounge (near E)** — best paid options for AA/oneworld passengers already in the right concourse.
- **Food:** **La Carreta** (Cuban, main terminal) and **La Sandwicherie (Concourse D)** — order ahead at meal peaks.
- **Quiet seating:** Upper level near **Gate J17** (South) when you're already airside in **H/J**.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **Metrorail / Tri-Rail:** Take the **MIA Mover** (Level 3) to **Miami Central Station** — **~3 min** ride; then Metrorail to downtown or Tri-Rail northbound.
- **Rental cars:** Same **MIA Mover** to the consolidated rental center — avoid the bus during **15:00–18:00** cruise-turnaround peaks if walking is feasible.
- **Parking:** Short-term garage above check-in; long-term lots require shuttle.
- **Uber/Lyft:** Arrivals level pickup; surge common **16:00–19:00**.
