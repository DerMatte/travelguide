# MIA Airport Guide

> Practical, no-fluff guide to moving through Miami International Airport fast and with minimal stress.

## Quick Facts
- American Airlines dominates; most international flights use concourses **D, E, and J**.
- One U-shaped terminal complex split into **North** (Concourse D), **Central** (E/F/G), and **South** (H/J) zones.
- **Most concourse-to-concourse transfers require exiting security**, walking on **Level 3**, and re-clearing TSA at your departure concourse ([MIA flight connections](https://www.miami-airport.com/flight_connections.asp)).
- **Airside exceptions (no re-screening):** **D ↔ E** via bridge near gates **D31/E2**; **H ↔ J** via walkway in the South Terminal.
- **MIA Mover** is a free automated train on **Level 3** connecting the terminal to the **Rental Car Center** and **Miami Central Station** (Metrorail/Tri-Rail) — **not** an airside concourse connector.
- **Skytrain** runs **within Concourse D only** (end-to-end), above the concourse.
- Busiest periods: early morning departures to Europe and evening arrivals from Latin America.
- Free Wi-Fi is reliable but login required every 2 hours.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Use the MIA app or airport website for real-time checkpoint wait times.
- CLEAR + PreCheck lanes are at the main checkpoint and usually under 10 minutes.
- Avoid the central checkpoint **6–8 a.m.** and **4–6 p.m.**; try the smaller **E** and **J** checkpoints when flying those concourses.
- Liquids in clear bags are strictly enforced; no exceptions at MIA.
- **International → US domestic:** you must collect bags, clear CBP, recheck, and pass TSA again — budget **2.5–3 h** minimum.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the MIA app before arrival for gate changes and security times.
- **Before booking a tight connection**, confirm whether your arrival and departure concourses share an airside link (**D–E** or **H–J** only). A **D → J** connection means **full exit + re-screening**.
- Use the **Skytrain** to cross long **Concourse D** distances quickly — it does not reach other concourses.
- For **D ↔ E** on one ticket, use the **airside bridge** near **D31/E2** instead of exiting to Level 3.
- **MIA Mover** is for rental cars and **Metrorail/Tri-Rail** at Miami Central Station — not for skipping between gates.
- Quietest seating is the upper level near **Gate J17** after security.
- Pre-order food via airport vendors when possible — pickup is faster than lines at La Carreta or La Sandwicherie.

## Terminals & Navigation
- All passenger processing happens in one building; concourses are lettered **D–J** across three terminal zones.
- **Level 2:** departures/check-in; **Level 1:** arrivals/baggage; **Level 3:** inter-terminal walkways and MIA Mover access.
- Walking from security to the farthest gate (**J**) takes **20–25 minutes** within the same concourse.
- International arrivals must clear customs then re-check bags and re-screen for domestic connections.
- **Recommended buffers:**
  - Same concourse or **D–E / H–J** airside link: **60–90 min**
  - Cross-concourse with re-screening: **90 min–2 h**
  - International → US domestic: **2.5–3 h**

## Lounges Food & Amenities
- American Airlines Admirals Club in **D** and the Centurion Lounge near **E** are the best paid options.
- Standout food: **La Carreta** (Cuban) in the main terminal and **La Sandwicherie** in concourse D.
- Quietest non-lounge spot is the seating area past Gate H after the moving walkway.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **MIA Mover** (free, Level 3) to **Miami Central Station** for **Metrorail** and **Tri-Rail** — downtown Miami via Tri-Rail ~**25 min**, $2.50+.
- Rental car center is a **3-minute MIA Mover** ride; avoid during cruise turnarounds (**3–6 p.m.**).
- Short-term parking garage is directly above check-in; long-term is cheaper but requires a shuttle.
- Uber/Lyft pickup is on the arrivals level; surge pricing is common **4–7 p.m.**
