# MIA Airport Guide

> Practical, no-fluff guide to moving through Miami International Airport fast — and not confusing the MIA Mover with a concourse shuttle.

## Quick Facts
- **Layout:** One building with **concourses D–J** grouped in **North (D)**, **Central (E, F, G)**, and **South (H, J)** areas ([Getting around MIA](https://www.miami-airport.com/getting-around-mia.asp)).
- **American Airlines** dominates **Concourse D** (Skytrain runs the length of D); international traffic also uses **E, F, J**.
- **Airside connections:** You can stay airside only between **D ↔ E** and **H ↔ J** on eligible domestic connections — **not** after international arrivals that require CBP ([Flight connections](https://www.miami-airport.com/flight_connections.asp)).
- **Most other concourse changes** require exiting the secure area and **re-clearing TSA** at the destination concourse checkpoint.
- **MIA Mover** is a **free automated train** from **Level 3** (between Dolphin and Flamingo garages) to the **Rental Car Center** and **Miami Intermodal Center** (Metrorail, Tri-Rail, Metrobus) — **not** for hopping between concourses ([Public transportation](https://www.miami-airport.com/public-transportation.asp)).
- **Level 3 moving walkways** connect concourses for **landside** walks; still plan **15–25 min** gate-to-gate after security.
- Busiest periods: early Europe departures and evening Latin America arrivals; free Wi-Fi requires periodic re-login.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Use the MIA app or airport website for real-time checkpoint wait times.
- **CLEAR + PreCheck** lanes are at the main checkpoint and usually under **10 minutes** off-peak.
- Avoid the central checkpoint **6–8 a.m.** and **4–6 p.m.**; try smaller **E** or **J** checkpoints when your gate is there.
- Liquids in clear bags are strictly enforced; standard US **3-1-1** rules apply.
- **International → US domestic:** You **must collect bags** and clear CBP before connecting — budget **2.5–3+ hours**.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the MIA app before arrival for gate changes and security times.
- **Concourse D:** Use the **Skytrain** (Level above D) for the long hike between remote D gates — saves minutes vs walking the full pier.
- **Do not use MIA Mover for gate connections** — it goes to rental cars and rail, not between D and J.
- For **D ↔ J** connections on one ticket, assume **new TSA** unless airline staff direct you to an airside path (rare except D–E / H–J pairs).
- Quietest seating is the upper level near **Gate J17** after security.
- **MIA Mover + Metrorail** beats the rental-car bus during **3–6 p.m.** cruise passenger rushes if you are heading downtown with light bags.
- Pre-order food via airport vendors when possible — pickup beats lines at **La Carreta** or **La Sandwicherie**.

## Terminals & Navigation
- **Departures Level 2**, **arrivals Level 1**, **connections Level 3** (walkways + MIA Mover access).
- Walking from security to the farthest **J** gate can take **20–25 minutes**.
- **International arrivals** clear customs on **Level 1 or 3** depending on concourse — follow purple signs; recheck bags when connecting domestically.
- **Recommended buffers (practical):**
  - Same concourse / allowed airside pair (D–E or H–J): **60–90 min**
  - Concourse change with new TSA: **2 h** floor, **2.5 h** peaks
  - International → domestic: **3 h** minimum

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- **American Admirals Club (D)** and **Centurion Lounge (near E)** are the best paid options for AA-heavy itineraries.
- **Standout food:** **La Carreta** (Cuban) in the main terminal and **La Sandwicherie** in **Concourse D**.
- Quietest non-lounge spot: seating past **Gate H** after the moving walkway.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **MIA Mover** (Level 3) → **Miami Intermodal Center** for **Metrorail** and **Tri-Rail**; Tri-Rail to downtown areas is useful but station placement varies by destination.
- Rental car center is a **~3 min** MIA Mover ride — avoid the curb bus during cruise turnarounds.
- Short-term parking garage is directly above check-in; long-term is cheaper but requires shuttle.
- Uber/Lyft pickup is on the arrivals level; surge pricing is common **4–7 p.m.**
