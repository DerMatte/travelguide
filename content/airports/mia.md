# MIA Airport Guide

> Practical, no-fluff guide to moving through Miami International Airport fast and with minimal stress.

## Quick Facts
- American Airlines dominates; most international flights use concourses **D**, **E**, and **J**.
- One campus split into **North (D)**, **Central (E/F/G)**, and **South (H/J)** — not one continuous airside concourse.
- **Airside without re-screening:** only **D↔E** and **H↔J** (and only when you are not arriving on an international flight that must clear CBP).
- **MIA Mover** is a free elevated train to the **Rental Car Center** and Miami Intermodal Center — **not** a concourse connector ([MIA services](https://www.miami-airport.com/services-and-amenities.asp)).
- Landside **Level 3 Skyride** moving walkways link the three terminal areas ([Getting around MIA](https://www.miami-airport.com/getting-around-mia.asp)).
- Busiest periods: early morning departures to Europe and evening arrivals from Latin America.
- Free Wi-Fi is reliable but login required every 2 hours.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Use the MIA app or airport website for real-time checkpoint wait times.
- CLEAR + PreCheck lanes are at the main checkpoint and usually under 10 minutes.
- Avoid the central checkpoint **6–8 a.m.** and **4–6 p.m.**; try the smaller **E** and **J** checkpoints when flying those concourses.
- Liquids in clear bags are strictly enforced; no exceptions at MIA.
- **Concourse changes** outside D↔E or H↔J mean a **new TSA line** — budget queue time, not just walking time.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the MIA app before arrival for gate changes and security times.
- **D to J (or any cross-cluster hop):** use **Level 3 Skyride** walkways landside, then re-clear security at J — the **MIA Mover will not** shortcut this.
- For early flights, check bags at the American Airlines remote bag drop on the arrivals level when the main counters are slammed.
- **Concourse D Skytrain** (airside, within D only) saves the long hike between remote D gates — elevators up from the concourse.
- Quietest seating is the upper level near Gate **J17** after security.
- Avoid the rental car center bus during **3–6 p.m.** cruise passenger rushes; walk to the Tri-Rail station instead.
- Pre-order food via airport vendors when possible—pickup is faster than lines at La Carreta or La Sandwicherie.

## Terminals & Navigation
- All passenger processing happens in one building complex; concourses are lettered **D–J**.
- Walking from security to the farthest gate (**J**) takes **20–25 minutes** within one concourse cluster.
- International arrivals must clear **CBP** then re-check bags and re-clear TSA for domestic connections.
- **Common mistake:** riding the **MIA Mover** expecting a concourse transfer — it only serves parking/rental cars.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- American Airlines Admirals Club in D and the Centurion Lounge near E are the best paid options.
- Standout food: La Carreta (Cuban) in the main terminal and La Sandwicherie in concourse D.
- Quietest non-lounge spot is the seating area past Gate H after the moving walkway.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Tri-Rail from the airport station reaches downtown Miami in 25 minutes for $2.50.
- Rental car center is a free 5-minute **MIA Mover** ride; avoid during cruise turnarounds.
- Short-term parking garage is directly above check-in; long-term is cheaper but requires a shuttle.
- Uber/Lyft pickup is on the arrivals level; surge pricing is common 4–7 p.m.
