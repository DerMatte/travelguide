# MUC Airport Guide

> One of Europe’s best-run hubs for Star Alliance connections — if you know which terminal you’re in and when passport control bites.

## Quick Facts
- **Terminals:** **Terminal 2** (Lufthansa Group + Star Alliance) with **Satellite** gates J/K/L; **Terminal 1** split into modules **A–E** (E arrivals-only). **MAC** (Munich Airport Center) links T1 and T2 landside and holds the S-Bahn station.
- **MCT (same ticket, experienced floor):**
  - **T2 → T2** (incl. Satellite via PTS): **60 min** sane; airline-published mins can be **45 min** on one ticket — works often, risky if inbound is late or gates are far apart.
  - **Schengen ↔ non-Schengen** (any terminal): add **15–30 min** for passport control; peak arrivals can push toward **75–90 min**.
  - **T1 ↔ T2:** treat **90 min** as minimum; **2 hours** safer in summer/holiday peaks — airside shuttle **5–7 min** plus walking and possible re-screening depending on routing.
- **Separate tickets / self-transfer:** not a quick connection airport between terminals — budget **3+ hours** landside (bags, check-in, security).
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free **"Free Wifi – Munich Airport"** — browser registration with email; Passngr app offers auto-login for repeat visitors (per airport site).
- **Liquids:** EU **100 ml** rule still applies in hand luggage; CT lanes at equipped checkpoints allow **devices and liquids up to 100 ml to stay in the bag** (not a free-for-all on bottle size).

## Security & Screening Tips
- **Terminal 2 is the easy mode:** Central security reopened with full **CT scanner** rollout (completed Apr 2025). Off-peak, most passengers clear quickly; **05:00–08:30** bank departures are the realistic pain window — add **15–25 min** buffer.
- **Terminal 1 is modular:** Each module (A/B/C/D) has its own checkpoint culture. **Module D**, the **new T1 Pier**, and **all of T2** have CT; older T1 modules were still being upgraded ahead of **summer 2026** — you may be sent to a **conventional** lane (laptops/liquids out).
- **Star Alliance Gold Track** at **both T1 and T2** security for eligible Gold / Star business or first on a member flight — show boarding pass at the marked lane ([Star Alliance Gold Track](https://www.staralliance.com/en/gold-track)).
- **Lufthansa First / HON / Senator:** Dedicated **First Class check-in + security** in T2 (not the general queue) — worth it when the main hall backs up ([Lufthansa at MUC](https://www.lufthansa.com/us/en/lufthansa-terminal-muenchen)).
- **No Lufthansa advance security time-slot booking at MUC** (unlike Frankfurt/Düsseldorf on Lufthansa’s list) — priority is status/cabin lanes and airport-driven fast paths, not app reservations.
- **T1 crowd hack:** If your module queue spikes, **follow staff redirects** to alternate checkpoints — airport explicitly asks passengers to switch when another lane is shorter.
- **Families / PRM / Sunflower:** T2 has a dedicated **inclusive** CT checkpoint — slower for solo speed-runners, better if you need space or assistance.
- **EasyPASS:** Use automated gates when eligible on **non-Schengen** flows — saves minutes vs. manual booths at peak banks.
- **Liquids on connections:** EU-origin liquids in the clear bag usually stay compliant for Schengen connections; arrivals from **outside the EU** must meet the bag rules before any Schengen connection — plan to buy airside or pack compliant.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **Connection signage first:** After landing, follow **connecting flights** signs immediately — don’t drift into arrivals shopping unless you need to clear immigration.
- **T2 Satellite trap:** Gates **J/K/L** are **after** central security via the **PTS** shuttle (every **4 min**, ~**1 min** ride, **04:00–24:00**). Landing at **G/H** and departing from **J** can cost **15–25 min** walking + PTS even without security — check **both** gate areas on your pass.
- **T1↔T2 airside:** Free **shuttle bus** between terminals for connecting passengers (**5–7 min** ride). Still allow **walking + passport** time — this is not a sub-30-minute terminal change in practice.
- **MAC is the spine:** Landside, almost everything (S-Bahn, Airbräu, many shops) funnels through **MAC Level 03** — learn it once and navigation gets easier.
- **Airbräu (landside MAC):** World’s first airport brewery — solid Bavarian food and fresh beer **before** security or on a long layover **without** lounge access. Overrated for a 45-minute connection; excellent for **90+ min** landside.
- **T1 module discipline:** Your boarding pass module letter matters — wrong module means a **long landside hike** (T1 is ~1.1 km end-to-end). Non-Schengen **B/C** often use **Level 05** passport gallery above security — don’t camp on Level 04.
- **New T1 Pier (from 21 Apr 2026):** Non-Schengen pier with **centralized CT security** — if your airline moved here, old B/C habits may be wrong; confirm gate on the airport app the day of travel.
- **Wi‑Fi friction:** Registration is quick but repetitive — use **Passngr** if you connect often; charge while waiting because **outlets compete** at busy gates.
- **Water:** No widely advertised airside refill network — bring an **empty bottle** and fill landside if staff allow, or buy after security (airport retail pricing). Don’t assume Frankfurt-style refill density.
- **Avoid:** Booking **35–45 min** self-transfer T1→T2 on separate tickets — airport transfer guidance treats that as reckless even when the airport is “efficient.”

## Terminals & Navigation
- **Terminal 2 layout:** Check-in **G (Schengen, Level 4)** and **H (non-Schengen, Level 5)**; **Senator/Business lounges Level 5**; central security then airside toward **G/H** or down to **PTS → Satellite J/K/L**.
- **Terminal 1 layout:** Linear **modules A–D** (+ **E** arrivals). **A/D** Schengen-heavy on Level 4; **B/C** non-Schengen with upstairs passport control; **~40 airlines** now use the **new Pier** for non-Schengen with centralized security.
- **Walking times (order-of-magnitude):**
  - MAC center ↔ T1 module: **5–15 min** depending on module
  - T2 main ↔ Satellite gate area: **10–20 min** after security including PTS
  - T1 airside ↔ T2 airside (shuttle + walks): budget **25–40 min** excluding queues
- **Recommended connection buffers (practical, not airline marketing):**
  - Same ticket, **T2 only**, Schengen–Schengen: **60 min** floor, **75 min** comfortable
  - Same ticket, **T2 + Satellite** or Schengen border crossing: **75–90 min**
  - Same ticket, **T1 ↔ T2**: **90 min** floor, **2 h** in peaks
  - Separate tickets: **3+ hours** minimum
- **Common mistakes:** Assuming **TBIT-style** re-security on every T2 connection (many stay airside on one ticket); entering **wrong T1 module**; missing that **PTS stops after midnight** (plan landside reroute if applicable).

## Lounges Food & Amenities
- **Lufthansa Senator / Business (T2):** The default good lounges for status — **Level 5** for Schengen/non-Schengen split; additional lounges in the **Satellite**. **Works best** off-peak; **avoid** tight connections during the **morning long-haul bank** when Senator fills with connecting premium traffic (many FF reports).
- **Lufthansa First (T2):** Separate check-in/security and First lounge — genuinely saves time vs. Senator when queues spike; not worth paying up unless you’re already in the cabin.
- **Pay-for / independent:** **Airport Lounge Europe / World**, **Bayern-Lounge** (T1 — Bavarian vibe, mixed reviews vs. Star lounges for food), **Emirates** (T1 Module C for eligible itineraries). Day-pass rules change — **check before travel**.
- **Food — actually good:**
  - **Airbräu** (MAC, landside): Best “only in Munich” meal; not airside.
  - **Dallmayr Bistro** (T2 airside, Gates G/H): Reliable coffee and bistro plates for a quick connection.
  - **Viktualien Stubn** (T2 Satellite, Gates K/L): Better sit-down Bavarian option if you’re already past PTS.
  - **T1 Pier** (new): Five dining concepts by airport operator — worth trying if you’re pinned to T1 non-Schengen; menus still settling post-opening.
- **Overrated / traps:** Generic MAC food court when you have lounge access in T2; buying bottled water airside at premium prices when you had time landside in MAC.
- **Power:** T2 **Recreation Areas** and Satellite quiet zones advertise **outlets/USB** — grab them right after security, not at the gate during a delay.
- **Pharmacy / essentials:** Airport pharmacy and **hygiene vending** exist landside/airside per facilities list — for adapters and travel-size after security, plan ahead in MAC shops.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **Fastest value to Munich center:** **S8** (via Ostbahnhof) or **S1** (via west suburbs) from **MAC underground station** — **~40 min** to Hauptbahnhof area per airport/MVV guidance; lines alternate for **~10 min** combined headway daytime.
- **Night / early:** **S8 runs 24/7** but **~40 min** intervals **01:00–04:00**; S1 has limited overnight service — check MVV journey planner for your exact time.
- **Lufthansa Express Bus:** Nonstop MAC ↔ Munich central area every **20 min** — handy with heavy bags or when S-Bahn is disrupted; **paid** (check current fare before travel).
- **Taxi / ride-hail:** **~30–45 min** to central Munich off-peak by road; rush hour on A9/A92 can blow past **60 min** — train often wins for Marienplatz/Hbf.
- **Parking insider:** Several remote lots include **free MVV ride** to terminals on the parking ticket (P41, P43, P44, P50, P51, P90, etc. per airport) — good for multi-day trips if you’re driving in.
- **P20 landside tip:** **€30+** same-day shopping/dining can make **first hour free** in P20 — only if you were eating/shopping anyway, not worth contorting your trip.
- **Deutsche Bahn desk:** MAC Level 03 for domestic/European rail tickets and MVV passes — useful if you’re connecting to **Munich Hbf** long-distance trains.
