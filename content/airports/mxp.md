# MXP Airport Guide

> Practical, time-saving intel for Milan Malpensa that cuts stress on arrival, transfer, and departure.

## Quick Facts
- T1 handles most legacy carriers and Star Alliance; T2 is Ryanair/easyJet dominant.
- Free shuttle runs 05:00-00:30 between terminals; 7-min ride.
- Malpensa Express (Terminal 1) is fastest rail link; €13 one-way.
- Currency exchange rates airside are poor; withdraw euros landside.
- Free Wi-Fi is reliable but requires captcha every 45 min.
- **Power:** Outlets at T1 gates A1–A10; T2 has almost none — charge landside or in lounge.
- **Water:** Limited refill fountains airside — buy landside or bring filled bottle through security (empty through, fill after).
- Baggage storage available only in T1 arrivals, €6-12 per bag.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Remove liquids and laptops at T1; trays are mandatory.
- Families and passengers over 70 can use priority lanes at both terminals.
- Avoid 06:00-08:00 and 17:00-19:00; lines exceed 40 min.
- No Clear or PreCheck equivalent; arrive with liquids already bagged.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the official MXP app for real-time gate and shuttle updates.
- If connecting T1-T2 with <90 min, request a cart at check-in; staff sometimes fast-track.
- Buy Malpensa Express tickets at machines landside to skip the airside queue.
- T1 airside has power outlets at gates A1-A10; T2 has almost none.
- For early flights, the Sheraton T1 has a 24 h bar accessible without a room key.
- When Malpensa Express is delayed, the €10 bus to Milan Centrale often departs sooner.
- Keep your boarding pass visible; security re-scans at the gate in T2.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T1 is the larger, more modern building; T2 feels like a low-cost shed.
- Walking between terminals takes 20-25 min via covered path; shuttle is faster.
- Schengen and non-Schengen are clearly separated in T1; watch signs after security.
- Baggage claim is fast in T1, slow in T2; budget extra 15 min at T2.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- T1 airside: Ristorante Bistrò for sit-down meals; good espresso and pasta.
- T2: Only generic grab-and-go; eat before security if possible.
- Quietest seating: T1 gates A50-A60 after 21:00; few flights depart there.
- No paid lounges worth the cost for most travelers; Priority Pass options are mediocre.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Malpensa Express (red line) is best for Centrale; blue line for Cadorna.
- Taxi flat rate to Milan center is €95; avoid unofficial drivers.
- Short-term parking P1/P2 costs €3.50/h; long-term P5/P6 is €12/day.
- Bus to Milan (Autostradale) departs every 20 min; €10 cash only.
