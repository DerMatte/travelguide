# ORD Airport Guide

> Fast, no-nonsense reference for moving through O'Hare with minimal wasted time.

## Quick Facts
- **United** dominates **Terminals 1 and 2**; **American** uses **Terminal 3**; **Terminal 5** handles nearly all international arrivals and many international departures.
- **T1–T3** are linked **airside on foot** (tunnels/walkways) — no ATS needed for domestic-to-domestic connections within that cluster.
- **T5 ↔ T1/T3:** There is **no routine all-day airside walkway**. Use the **Terminal Transfer Bus (TTB)** **11:30 a.m.–9:30 p.m.** (every **15 min**, boarding pass required) between **T5 Gate M13** and **T1 Gate C18B** or **T3 Gates G17/K20** ([TTB info](https://www.flychicago.com/ohare/ServicesAmenities/services/Pages/tbt.aspx)). **9:30 p.m.–11:30 a.m.:** exit security, ride the free **ATS** landside, **re-clear TSA** at the departure terminal.
- **International → US domestic:** Everyone clears **CBP**, **claims checked bags**, may **recheck**, then **ATS + TSA** to T1/T2/T3 — plan **2.5–3+ hours** minimum ([connecting traveler](https://www.flychicago.com/ohare/tofrom/connecting)).
- **ATS** runs **24/7** between all terminals and the rental-car center (~**4 min** ride + wait).
- Security peaks **6–9 a.m.** and **3–8 p.m.**; **TSA PreCheck** and **CLEAR** at all terminals.

## Security & Screening Tips
- **CLEAR + TSA PreCheck** at Terminal 1 is usually the fastest combined lane when eligible.
- Avoid the main **Terminal 3** checkpoint **5–7 p.m.**; try the **satellite checkpoint near Gate K8** when open.
- **Terminal 5 international arrivals:** **Global Entry** kiosks cut CBP time materially on heavy banks.
- **TTB passengers** skip a second TSA when staying airside on eligible **T5 ↔ T1/T3** connections during **TTB hours** — everyone else re-screens after ATS.
- Peak congestion: arrive at security **75–90 min** before departure with PreCheck; add **30+ min** without it at peaks.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Walk the **T2–T3 tunnel** to bypass ATS when moving between United and American during rush hour.
- Use the **post-security walkway T1 ↔ T2** (~**8–10 min**) instead of ATS when both flights are in the United cluster.
- **4–6 a.m.** often has the shortest security lines even on busy travel days.
- **Fly Chicago app** for ATS and security wait times (updates every few minutes).
- **Do not assume** you can stay airside from **T5** overnight or before **11:30 a.m.** — plan **ATS + TSA** outside TTB hours.
- **United Polaris lounge** (T1, near C18) sometimes sells day passes after **5 p.m.** when under capacity — best odds after **6 p.m.**

## Terminals & Navigation
- **Terminal 1 (United):** gates B and C; **Terminal 2 (United Express):** E and F; **Terminal 3 (American):** H, K, L; **Terminal 5 (international):** M.
- **T1 ↔ T3** via tunnel: **12–15 min** walk; ATS: **~4 min** + wait (landside, requires re-screening from T5).
- **TTB (airside T5 only):** **T1 pickup C18B** (B1 stop temporarily closed per airport notices), **T3 G17 & K20**, **T5 M13**.
- **Common mistake:** Booking a tight **T5 → T1/T3** connection that lands outside **TTB hours** or after an international arrival without baggage-recheck time.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- **United Polaris Lounge** (T1, near C18) — best paid option with showers when eligible.
- **Garrett Popcorn** in T3 (near H4) and T1 (near B8) — reliable local snack.
- **Tortas Frontera** (T1 B8, T3 K4) — best sit-down meal under **$20**.
- **Yoga room** in T3 (near K12) — free, 24/7, rarely crowded.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **CTA Blue Line** to downtown: **$5**, **24/7**, **45–55 min** to the Loop.
- Rideshare surges often hit **Terminal 1** — **T2/T3** curb can be faster at peaks.
- **Economy parking ~$15/day** + **5-min ATS**; main garage **~$40/day** for short trips.
- Leave the rental-car center by **6:30 a.m.** to beat weekday morning security rushes.
