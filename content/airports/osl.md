# OSL Airport Guide

> One terminal, fast train links, and predictable security make OSL easy to navigate when you know the peaks.

## Quick Facts
- Single terminal with all check-in, security, and gates in one building.
- **MCT (same ticket):** **40 min** Schengen–Schengen floor; **60 min** with non-Schengen passport control — OSL is efficient but peaks bite.
- Airport Express train reaches Oslo Central Station in 20 minutes.
- Dominant carriers are SAS and Norwegian; most European flights are Schengen.
- Free Wi-Fi is fast and reliable throughout the terminal.
- **Power:** Long rows of seats opposite gates 40–50 — usually empty outlets.
- **Water:** Refill fountains post-security near gates 20 and 40 — bring an empty bottle.
- Baggage storage and shower facilities available landside near arrivals.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Lines are longest 06:00-09:00 and 16:00-19:00; use the Avinor app for live wait times.
- Liquids in 100 ml containers must go in a clear bag; no PreCheck equivalent exists.
- Families and passengers with reduced mobility get a dedicated lane—ask staff at the entrance.
- Avoid the leftmost lanes if you have only carry-on; they are slower.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Board the Airport Express at Oslo S platform 13-14 for the fastest seats; buy tickets on the app to skip machines.
- Charge devices at the long rows of seats opposite gates 40-50—usually empty.
- Buy a coffee and pastry at the central food hall after security instead of gate kiosks to save 30-40 %.
- Use the quiet seating area near gate 1 when flights are delayed; power outlets and fewer announcements.
- Download the Widerøe or SAS app for same-day standby upgrades on domestic legs.
- Walk the full length of the terminal once to locate the best bathrooms (cleanest near gates 30-35).
- If renting a car, pre-book the cheaper remote lots and take the free shuttle—saves 150-200 NOK versus terminal parking.

## Terminals & Navigation
- One terminal means no connections between buildings; gates are numbered 1-50 with a short walk from security.
- Schengen and non-Schengen areas are clearly divided after security; allow 10 minutes to move between them.
- Moving walkways run the full length—use them when carrying luggage to gates beyond 40.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- The SAS Lounge near gate 34 offers decent hot food and showers for eligible passengers.
- Standout food post-security is the salmon and reindeer dishes in the central food court.
- Quiet seating zones with armrests are located between gates 10-20 and near gate 1.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Airport Express train is fastest and most reliable to Oslo; buy return tickets for a discount.
- Vy regional trains are cheaper but slower and require a change at Oslo S during peak hours.
- Short-term parking directly outside arrivals costs 80 NOK for the first 30 minutes; use the P1 or P2 garages for longer stays.
- Taxi ranks are well organized but expensive—book via the app to avoid overcharging.
