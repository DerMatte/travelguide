# PHL Airport Guide

> Fast, no-fluff intel for the American hub that still surprises travelers with long security lines and awkward terminal walks.

## Quick Facts
- American Airlines operates the vast majority of flights from Terminals A, B, and C.
- Terminals A–F are connected post-security; the farthest gate-to-gate walk is about 12 minutes.
- International arrivals clear customs and immigration only in Terminal A.
- TSA PreCheck lanes exist at A-West, B, and E; CLEAR is at the same checkpoints.
- SEPTA Regional Rail Airport Line runs every 30 minutes to 30th Street Station.
- Short-term parking garages sit directly above each terminal; long-term lots require a shuttle.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Lines at A-West and B are longest weekday mornings 6–9am; E is usually faster for evening departures.
- PreCheck and CLEAR cut average wait to under 10 minutes; standard lanes can exceed 30.
- Liquids in a single clear quart bag are still enforced; no exceptions at PHL.
- Families and wheelchair users move fastest through A-West checkpoint.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the official PHL app for live checkpoint wait times before you leave the car.
- If connecting from an A gate to an F gate, walk instead of waiting for the shuttle.
- Terminal D has the least crowded seating after security near gate D1.
- Grab a La Colombe coffee in D before a long sit in B or C; lines are shorter.
- Use the cell-phone lot on the arrivals level when picking someone up; it’s free for 30 minutes.
- Check your boarding pass gate on the app even after you clear security—American sometimes moves flights between B and C.
- Fill a water bottle after security
