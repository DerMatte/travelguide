# PHX Airport Guide

> Fast, no-fluff reference for clearing PHX with minimal wasted time.

## Quick Facts
- Terminals 2, 3, and 4 only; all connected by free Sky Train.
- American Airlines operates primarily from Terminal 4; Southwest from Terminal 3.
- Sky Train runs every 3-5 minutes and stops at 44th Street for Valley Metro light rail.
- TSA PreCheck lanes exist in every terminal; CLEAR is available in Terminals 3 and 4.
- Outdoor walking distances can exceed 10 minutes between parking and check-in in summer heat.
- No airside connection between Terminal 3 and Terminal 4.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Standard wait times stay under 20 minutes except during 6-8 a.m. and 4-7 p.m. weekday banks.
- Use the TSA app or airport website for current lane status before arrival.
- PreCheck and CLEAR are fastest in Terminal 4; Terminal 3 PreCheck can back up during Southwest morning departures.
- Liquids in quart bags are still enforced; no known relaxed rules.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Park in Terminal 4 East Economy and ride the free shuttle if you want covered parking without garage rates.
- After clearing security in Terminal 4, walk to the far end of concourse D for shorter lines at the B concourse people-mover.
- Download the PHX app and enable push notifications for gate changes; they appear before airline apps.
- If connecting from Terminal 3 to Terminal 4, ride the Sky Train one stop to 44th Street and back to skip the longer indoor walk.
- Fill reusable bottles at the post-security fountains near gates B24 and D7; both are rarely crowded.
- For early-morning flights, check in at Terminal 4 even on Southwest if you already have a boarding pass; the Terminal 3 curb is often more congested.
- Rideshare pickup is on the north side of Terminal 4; avoid the south side unless your driver specifically routes there.
- Keep a light layer in your carry-on; jet bridges can be 20-30 °F cooler than the 110 °F tarmac in summer.

## Terminals & Navigation
- Sky Train runs continuously between Terminals 2, 3, 4 and 44th Street; allow 8-12 minutes between 3 and 4.
- Terminal 4 is the largest and contains most amenities; Terminal 2 is small and used mainly by smaller carriers.
- Walking between Terminal 3 and 4 security checkpoints is not possible airside; you must exit and re-clear.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- The Centurion Lounge in Terminal 4 (near D7) is the strongest paid option; access rules match standard Amex policy.
- Terminal 4 has the better food selection; standout spots include Blue Corn Café and a post-security Starbucks with seating.
- Quietest seating is usually at the far end of Terminal 4 concourse A or near gate D15.
- No standout lounges in Terminal 3; most passengers use gate seating.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Economy parking at Terminal 4 is the best value for multi-day trips; Terminal 3 economy is smaller and fills faster.
- Light rail from 44th Street reaches downtown Phoenix in about 20 minutes; runs every 12-20 minutes.
- Rideshare pickup is clearly signed on the departure level; wait times are shortest on the north curb of Terminal 4.
- Rental cars are reached via free shuttle from the Terminal 4 baggage claim level; allow 10-15 minutes.
