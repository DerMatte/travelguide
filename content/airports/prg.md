# PRG Airport Guide

> Fast, no-nonsense guide to navigating Prague’s airport with minimal stress and wasted time.

## Quick Facts
- Terminals 1 (non-Schengen) and 2 (Schengen) are linked airside by a 8-10 minute walk.
- Ryanair and Wizz Air dominate low-cost traffic; most departures from T2.
- Security is manual and can back up quickly during morning and evening rushes.
- Currency exchange rates inside are poor—use ATMs or exchange before arrival.
- Public transport to center takes 30-40 min via bus 119 + metro.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Expect 15-25 min average; arrive at the checkpoint no later than 90 min before departure.
- Remove liquids and laptops—staff are strict and slow to process bins.
- Families and elderly get priority lanes; use them if eligible to skip the main queue.
- Avoid 6:30-8:30am and 5:30-7:30pm windows when possible.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the prg.aero app for live gate changes before the boards update.
- Buy a tram ticket at the machine outside arrivals to skip the bus driver cash hassle.
- Use the quiet seating area near gate B5 in T2 when all other gates are crowded.
- Fill a water bottle after security—T2 has free fountains near the lounges.
- If connecting Schengen to non-Schengen, clear security in T2 then walk to T1 rather than re-clearing.
- Park in P3 short-term for quick drop-offs; P1/P2 fill fast and cost more.
- Grab a trdelník at the stand near arrivals if you need a quick snack before transport.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T2 is newer, cleaner, and has shorter gate walks—prefer it when your airline allows.
- Airside connection between T1 and T2 is straightforward but adds 10 min minimum.
- Follow yellow signage for transfers; blue for arrivals/exits.
- Moving walkways are reliable but often crowded during peak arrivals.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- Potrefená Husa in both terminals offers solid Czech meals and beer pre-security.
- T2 post-security has a small but decent food court; avoid the overpriced coffee stands.
- The Erste Premier Lounge in T2 is the best paid option if you have access or want to pay.
- Free showers are available in T2 near the lounges—useful after long flights.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Bus 119 to Nádraží Veleslavín metro is cheapest and fastest to the center.
- Taxi stands are regulated; use the official desk to avoid rip-offs—expect 500-700 CZK to center.
- P3 parking is best for short stays; reserve online for lower rates.
- Avoid Uber/Bolt at arrivals—they often get blocked and add wait time.
