# SAW Airport Guide

> Fast, no-nonsense guide to Sabiha Gökçen for travelers who want to move through quickly and avoid common delays.

## Quick Facts
- Single terminal handles every flight; no separate domestic/international buildings.
- Pegasus and AnadoluJet dominate; Turkish Airlines uses IST almost exclusively.
- 35 km from central Istanbul on the Asian side; traffic on the E-80 can double travel time.
- Free Wi-Fi is reliable but requires captcha every 45 minutes.
- **Power:** Benches opposite gate 15 have plentiful outlets — best free charging spot.
- **Water:** Limited airside refill — eat/drink landside before security; bring empty bottle.
- Currency exchange and ATMs are concentrated near the main entrance.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Use the dedicated online check-in kiosks to skip the first bag-drop queue.
- Liquids are strictly 100 ml; no exceptions even for duty-free purchases after screening.
- Families and elderly travelers get fast-track lanes—ask staff if the line exceeds 20 people.
- Avoid the central security checkpoint between 07:00-09:00 and 19:00-21:00.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the SAW app before arrival; it shows real-time gate and bus stand numbers.
- If your flight is delayed, stay landside longer—food and seating are better and cheaper.
- Pegasus boarding starts 30 minutes before departure; arriving at the gate early gets you better overhead space.
- Use the leftmost security lane when all are open—it usually processes faster.
- For early flights, the 24-hour McDonald’s landside is the only reliable hot meal option after midnight.
- Keep your boarding pass visible on your phone; staff scan it at multiple points.
- The short-stay parking garage has an elevator directly into departures—use it when raining.
- If connecting to a SAW-IST bus, buy the ticket at the information desk to avoid airport taxi markups.

## Terminals & Navigation
- One terminal, two levels: departures upstairs, arrivals downstairs. Walking time gate-to-gate never exceeds 12 minutes.
- Follow yellow signs for gates 1-10 (left) and 11-20 (right); the central atrium is the only meeting point.
- No train between SAW and IST—budget 90-120 minutes by road or Havaist bus.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- The only worthwhile paid lounge is the Pegasus Lounge near gate 7; free for business class or paid entry.
- Landside: Simit Sarayı and a decent Turkish breakfast spot before security.
- Airside: only fast-casual chains and one overpriced café—eat before clearing security.
- Quietest seating is the row of benches opposite gate 15; power outlets are plentiful there.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Havaist H-2 bus to Taksim runs every 30 minutes, 45-70 min, ~€4.
- Airport taxis use fixed rates; insist on the meter or pre-agreed price to avoid overcharging.
- Short-term parking (P1) is expensive; long-term (P3) is cheaper but requires a 5-minute shuttle.
- Metro M4 reaches Kadıköy in 30 minutes—best option if heading to the Asian side.
