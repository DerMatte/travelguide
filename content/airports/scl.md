# SCL Airport Guide

> Plan extra time for security and use the free shuttle for any terminal change.

## Quick Facts
- T1 serves all international flights; T2 is domestic only.
- LATAM operates the majority of flights and uses T1 exclusively.
- Free shuttle runs every 10-15 minutes between T1 and T2.
- No TSA PreCheck or equivalent; all passengers go through standard screening.
- Currency exchange and SIM cards available landside in both terminals.
- Buses to downtown (Centropuerto, TurBus) cost ~CLP 2,000 and run 24/7.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Expect 45-90 minute waits at T1 peaks; use the leftmost lanes if available.
- Remove liquids and electronics completely—agents are strict.
- Families and elderly get priority lanes; ask staff if eligible.
- Avoid 5-8 a.m. and 6-9 p.m. if your schedule allows.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the official SCL app before arrival for live gate and delay alerts.
- Buy a local SIM at the Claro or Entel stand in T1 arrivals for cheap data.
- Use the free shuttle even for short connections; walking the full route takes 20+ minutes.
- Fill water bottles after security in T1 near gate 15—fountains are reliable.
- Book LATAM flights with long layovers; the airline lounge in T1 is accessible with day passes.
- Exit immigration quickly by using the e-gates if you have a biometric passport.
- Park in the short-term lot only for pickups under 30 minutes; long-term is cheaper but farther.
- Carry small CLP bills for bus fares; drivers rarely give change for large notes.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T1 and T2 sit 800 m apart; the free shuttle is faster than walking.
- All international arrivals and departures occur in T1; domestic uses T2.
- Minimum connection time is 90 minutes for international-to-domestic.
- Signage is clear in English and Spanish; follow yellow “Conexiones” signs.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- LATAM Lounge in T1 offers good showers and food; day passes available at the door.
- T1 departures food court has solid empanadas at Donde la Cuca and strong coffee at Juan Valdez.
- Quiet seating clusters near gates 20-30 and the far end of T2.
- Free Wi-Fi is fast but requires captcha every 45 minutes.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Centropuerto bus to Los Héroes metro station runs every 10-20 minutes, 24/7.
- Official taxis and Uber wait outside arrivals; Uber is usually cheaper.
- Short-term parking costs CLP 2,500 per hour; long-term drops to CLP 12,000 per day.
- Avoid street parking near the terminal—towing is common.
