# SEA Airport Guide

> Fast, no-fluff reference for clearing SEA with minimal stress and wasted time.

## Quick Facts
- One terminal, six concourses (A, B, C, D, N, S); N and S are satellites.
- Alaska Airlines dominates N/S; Delta uses A-C.
- Link light rail runs every 8-15 min from the airport station directly to downtown.
- TSA PreCheck lanes at the main checkpoint; CLEAR kiosks on the left.
- International arrivals require re-clearing security for all connections.
- No moving walkways between satellites and main concourses after security.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Main checkpoint opens 24/7; longest waits 5:30-8:30am and 4-7pm.
- PreCheck + CLEAR is fastest; standard PreCheck still beats regular line most days.
- Avoid the far-right regular lanes during morning rush.
- Liquids in quart bags are strictly enforced; no exceptions noted.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the Port of Seattle app for live security wait times before you leave the hotel.
- If connecting from N/S to A-D, budget 25-30 min and expect to re-clear security.
- Fill water bottles at the multiple stations past security instead of buying inside.
- Alaska MVP and Delta Comfort+ passengers can use the shorter dedicated PreCheck lanes when open.
- The Central Terminal has the shortest walks to most food and restrooms.
- For early flights, the Link light rail starts at 5am; earlier arrivals need rideshare or parking.
- Avoid the rental car shuttle if returning a car—use the direct garage skybridge instead.

## Terminals & Navigation
- All passenger areas are connected pre-security; post-security requires tram or 10-15 min walk to satellites.
- N and S satellites have no airside walkway to A-D—plan accordingly for tight connections.
- Walking time from security to farthest gate (N or S) is 12-18 min at normal pace.
- Signs are clear but gate changes are common; re-check boards after security.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- The Club at SEA (paid day pass) in the main terminal is the most reliable lounge option.
- Standout food: Anthony's (C) for seafood, Beecher's mac & cheese (Central), and the ramen counter in N.
- Quietest seating is usually the far end of Concourse A or the window seats near gate C16.
- No free showers; paid day rooms available near the main terminal.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Link light rail is cheapest and fastest to downtown; $3 with ORCA card.
- Rideshare pickup is on the 5th floor of the garage—follow signs, not the departures curb.
- Garage parking fills by 8am on weekdays; reserve in advance or use the remote economy lot with shuttle.
- Avoid the cell phone lot during peak arrival banks—it fills and closes.
