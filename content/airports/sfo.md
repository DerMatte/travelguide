# SFO Airport Guide

> One-page reference that cuts through SFO's terminal sprawl and United-heavy traffic patterns to save time and reduce stress.

## Quick Facts
- Four terminals (T1, T2, T3, International) linked by free AirTrain running 24/7.
- United dominates T3 and the International Terminal; Alaska uses T2; Delta uses T1.
- International Terminal handles both long-haul and some domestic flights.
- BART station sits directly under the International Terminal with trains every 10-20 minutes.
- CLEAR and TSA PreCheck lanes exist at T1, T2, T3, and International checkpoints.
- Rental cars and rideshares require AirTrain ride to the Rental Car Center or departure level curbs.

## Security & Screening Tips
- T3 United checkpoint is the busiest; arrive early for 7-9 a.m. and 4-7 p.m. departures.
- Use the airport website's live wait-time display before choosing a checkpoint.
- PreCheck and CLEAR are fastest at T2 and International Terminal when T3 is backed up.
- Families and travelers with liquids should avoid the smaller T1 checkpoint during peak hours.
- Check TSA.gov for any liquid or device rule changes before travel.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Board the AirTrain at the first stop after security in T3 to reach International Terminal gates faster than walking.
- Refill bottles at the T3 post-security stations near gates 70-90 instead of buying water.
- When United shows a gate change to the International Terminal, take the AirTrain one stop rather than walking the long corridor.
- Use the departure-level cell phone lot on the north side of the airport for short waits; it updates via signs.
- Download the FlySFO app for gate alerts; it often updates before airline apps during United bank changes.
- For early flights, park in the International Terminal garage and walk to T3 via AirTrain to avoid T3 garage congestion.
- Avoid the T3 food court between 11 a.m.-1 p.m.; walk one AirTrain stop to T2 for shorter lines.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T2 and T3 sit side-by-side; use AirTrain for any connection involving checked bags.
- International Terminal A and G gates require the most walking; budget 12-15 minutes from security.
- All terminals connect airside via AirTrain; no need to exit and re-clear security for domestic connections.
- Common mistake: following signs for "United gates" without confirming T3 vs. International Terminal.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- United Club in T3 offers decent food but gets crowded; the International Terminal United Club is quieter.
- T2 has the strongest food options post-security, including a reliable Peet's and solid grab-and-go.
- Quiet seating exists near gates 50-59 in T3 after the main concourse crowds thin.
- No standout paid lounges for non-United passengers; focus on gate areas instead.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- BART is fastest and cheapest to downtown; buy a ticket before boarding to avoid surcharges.
- Rideshares pick up on the departure level; expect 10-20 minute waits during evening rush.
- Long-term parking in the International Terminal garage is usually easier to access than T3.
- Check the airport website for current construction impacts on the departure-level curb before arrival.
