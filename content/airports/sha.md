# SHA Airport Guide

> Compact domestic airport 13 km from central Shanghai—fast in/out but tight on international amenities.

## Quick Facts
- Two terminals (T1 domestic-focused, T2 handles majority of flights including all international)
- Main carriers: China Eastern, Spring Airlines, Juneyao, Air China
- Metro Lines 2 and 10 reach People's Square in 25-35 minutes
- Free shuttle runs every 10 minutes between T1 and T2
- High-speed rail to PVG (Pudong) departs every 20 minutes from T2
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free airport Wi‑Fi — Chinese phone verification; bring VPN for foreign sites.
- **Power:** T2 Level 4 observation deck area; scarce at gates — charge landside if you have time.
- **Water:** Refill fountains in T2 post-security; limited in T1.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Domestic security opens 2.5 hours pre-departure; arrive at least 2 hours early during weekday mornings.
- No TSA PreCheck equivalent—expect full ID + boarding pass scan plus liquids in clear bag.
- Avoid 7-9 am and 5-7 pm windows when business travelers create 30-45 minute queues.
- Power banks over 100 Wh must be declared; lithium batteries in carry-on only.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the Shanghai Airport WeChat mini-program for live gate and delay alerts before you arrive.
- If connecting to PVG, take the high-speed rail from T2 basement instead of taxi—35 minutes, ¥50, no traffic.
- Metro Line 2 is fastest to city center but skips during rush hour; use Line 10 as backup.
- T2 Level 4 has the best quiet seating near the observation deck—rarely crowded after 9 pm.
- Pre-order taxi via Didi app inside arrivals to skip the official taxi queue.
- Free luggage wrapping stations are at T2 departures; use them for checked bags on international legs.
- Keep metro card topped up—SHA machines accept WeChat Pay/Alipay only.

## Terminals & Navigation
T2 is the main terminal for most travelers. T1 and T2 are 1.2 km apart; free shuttle takes 5 minutes. Walking between terminals is not practical with luggage. Allow 15 minutes minimum for the shuttle plus security re-screen if changing terminals.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
Din Tai Fung (T2, airside and landside) is the standout for consistent quality. T2 has a small 24-hour convenience store post-security. No standout paid lounges for non-premium passengers; China Eastern lounge is accessible only with business or Star Alliance Gold. Quietest seating is near the T2 observation deck on Level 4.

## Ground Transport & Parking
Taxi to central Shanghai costs ¥40-60 and takes 25-40 minutes depending on traffic. Metro is cheapest and reliable outside rush hour. Short-term parking is ¥10/hour in T2 garage; long-term cheaper at remote lots with free shuttle. Book Didi in advance during evening peaks.
