# SVO Airport Guide

> Fast reference for the main Aeroflot hub: terminal moves, security timing, and ground transport that actually works.

## Quick Facts
- Aeroflot’s primary base; Terminal B handles most domestic flights, C the majority of international.
- Terminals B–C–D/E/F are linked airside by free shuttle buses every 15 minutes.
- Aeroexpress rail reaches Belorussky Station in 35–50 minutes; taxis cost 2–3× more in traffic.
- No TSA PreCheck equivalent; all passengers go through standard Russian security.
- Free Wi-Fi needs phone verification; power outlets are scarce in B and older sections of D.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Arrive 2.5 hours early for flights from C or D; lines exceed 45 minutes 05:00–08:00 and 18:00–22:00.
- Remove liquids and electronics at every checkpoint; no Clear-style fast track exists.
- Families and passengers with reduced mobility can use the shorter “special assistance” lane—ask staff at entry.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the Aeroexpress app and buy tickets in advance; machines at the station often have card-reader failures.
- If connecting from international C to domestic B, stay airside and take the free shuttle instead of exiting and re-clearing security.
- Terminal C landside has the only decent 24-hour coffee (Starbucks) before security; buy before you go airside.
- Quiet sleeping chairs are on the upper level of C near gate 26; they are rarely full after midnight.
- Keep a printed boarding pass—mobile versions sometimes fail Russian barcode scanners.
- For early flights, the Capsule Hotel in D is cheaper than lounge access and includes a shower.
- Avoid the official taxi desk; use the Yandex Go app once you have Wi-Fi or data.

## Terminals & Navigation
- B (domestic) and C (international) sit 1 km apart; the free airside bus takes 8–12 minutes.
- D, E, and F are connected by walking corridors (5–10 minutes between farthest gates).
- Common mistake: exiting to landside for a connection between B and C—requires new security and adds 40+ minutes.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- The best paid lounge is in C near gate 15 (Priority Pass accepted); B lounge is small and often full.
- Solid food options exist only landside in C (cafés and a small supermarket); airside choices are mostly grab-and-go.
- Free showers are available in C near the quiet zone; bring your own towel.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Aeroexpress is fastest and cheapest to central Moscow; trains run every 30–60 minutes.
- Official taxis from the desk cost 2,500–4,000 RUB; Yandex Go is usually half that once outside the terminal.
- Short-term parking at C is 300 RUB/hour; long-term lots are cheaper but require a free shuttle.
