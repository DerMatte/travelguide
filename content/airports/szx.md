# SZX Airport Guide

> Fast, no-nonsense guide to moving through Shenzhen Bao'an without wasting time on bad advice.

## Quick Facts
- Terminal 3 handles 95% of traffic; T1 and T2 are small domestic satellites.
- Shenzhen Airlines, Spring, and Hainan dominate; few foreign carriers.
- Direct Metro Line 11 to Futian and Luohu; 30-40 minutes to downtown.
- No airside transfer between international and domestic without exiting security.
- High-speed rail station inside the airport complex for Hong Kong and Guangzhou.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Expect full bag X-ray plus explosive trace detection on all liquids and electronics.
- Remove laptops and power banks; 100 ml liquid rule is enforced strictly.
- Peak delays hit 7-9am and 5-8pm; use the far-left lanes in T3 for slightly shorter lines.
- Foreign passports trigger extra manual checks—have boarding pass and passport ready.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the Shenzhen Airport WeChat mini-program before arrival for live gate info.
- After security on 4F, turn right for quieter seating near gates 40-50.
- Metro exit is signposted “地铁” on arrivals level; buy a Shenzhen Tong card to skip queues.
- If connecting to Hong Kong, take the high-speed rail from the airport station instead of a taxi.
- Free charging lockers are available near the 4F food court—bring your own cable.
- Avoid the official taxi queue at rush hour; use the Didi app pickup zone on the departures level.
- T3 has free luggage wrapping machines near check-in rows 20-30.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T3 is a single large building; walking end-to-end takes 12-15 minutes.
- International departures are on the left side of T3; domestic on the right.
- Free inter-terminal shuttle runs every 10 minutes but is rarely needed.
- Clear English signage exists, but gate numbers change frequently—double-check the mini-program.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- Priority Pass lounges in T3 are average; the one near gate 35 is least crowded.
- Local favorites on 4F include Heytea and several Cantonese congee spots.
- Quietest seating is the row of benches between gates 55-65.
- Showers are available in the paid lounges only; no free options.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Metro Line 11 is fastest and cheapest to central Shenzhen.
- Airport bus routes are useful only if your hotel is far from a metro station.
- Short-term parking is expensive; long-term lots are a 10-minute free shuttle away.
- Didi pickup is on the upper departures level to avoid the taxi scrum.
