# SZX Airport Guide

> Fast reference for the single terminal that matters and the fastest ways in and out of Shenzhen.

## Quick Facts
- Terminal 3 handles 95%+ of all passenger traffic; T1 and T2 are effectively mothballed for most travelers.
- Shenzhen Airlines and Spring Airlines dominate; Star Alliance and oneworld flights also use T3.
- Direct Metro Line 11 link to Futian and Luohu; no airport express train.
- International arrivals require full immigration and baggage reclaim before any connections.
- Free Wi-Fi requires Chinese phone number verification; VPN recommended for foreign SIMs.
- No airside transit without visa for most nationalities.

## Security & Screening Tips
- All passengers face full body scan and liquid restrictions (100 ml rule strictly enforced).
- Remove laptops and liquids into separate bins; power banks must be carried on.
- Peak congestion hits 7-9am and 6-8pm; aim for shoulder periods if possible.
- No expedited screening programs available to foreign travelers.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the airline WeChat mini-program before arrival for real-time gate info and mobile boarding passes.
- Use the leftmost security lanes in T3 departures when queues are long; they often move faster.
- Buy SIM or eSIM at the arrivals level vending machines to avoid the long China Mobile counter lines.
- If connecting domestically after an international arrival, stay airside only if your airline tags bags through; otherwise collect and re-check.
- Metro Line 11 machines accept Alipay and WeChat Pay; have one ready or use cash at the staffed window.
- Quiet seating areas exist near gates 50-60 in T3 after the main food court.
- Avoid the official taxi queue at peak times; licensed rides via Didi app are faster and cheaper when surge is low.
- Power outlets are scarce near gates; bring a power bank or sit in the restaurants that provide them.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T3 is a single large building with domestic (T3C/D) and international (T3G) zones connected airside.
- Walking time from security to farthest gate is 12-15 minutes; moving walkways are reliable.
- No need to change terminals for almost any itinerary.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- DragonPass and Priority Pass lounges in T3 are functional but crowded; the one near gate 35 is usually quieter.
- Best food options post-security are the congee and hand-pulled noodle stalls on the upper departures level.
- Free drinking water fountains are located near most restrooms after security.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Metro Line 11 is the fastest and cheapest option into Shenzhen; trains run every 5-8 minutes.
- Official taxis are metered; insist on the meter or use the Didi app for fixed airport pricing.
- Long-term parking is available but check current rates on the airport site before committing.
