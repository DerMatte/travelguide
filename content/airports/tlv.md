# TLV Airport Guide

> Strict security and early arrival rules make TLV predictable once you know the sequence.

## Quick Facts
- Terminal 3 handles every international departure; Terminal 1 is for domestic and a few LCCs.
- Expect two full security screenings plus bag X-ray before check-in.
- High-speed rail to Tel Aviv HaHagana station costs 15 ₪ and runs 5 a.m.–11 p.m.
- No TSA PreCheck or equivalent; every passenger receives the same process.
- Free Wi-Fi is reliable throughout both terminals.
- Currency exchange and ATMs sit only in the public area before security.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Begin the first security line at least three hours before departure; two hours is the minimum for off-peak.
- Have passport, ticket, and any Israeli entry stamps ready for the initial questioning.
- Liquids over 100 ml must be declared and are usually confiscated; pack them in checked bags.
- Families and passengers over 70 can request the shorter “assisted” lane—ask an officer.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Print boarding passes at home; self-service kiosks after security are slow.
- Use the free luggage-wrapping stations on level 2 if you have time; costs 60 ₪ at the paid stands.
- Download the IAA app before arrival for real-time queue lengths and gate changes.
- Charge devices in the public hall; power outlets are scarce after the first checkpoint.
- Buy a local SIM at the 013 or Partner kiosk in arrivals if staying more than two days.
- Avoid the official taxi line at rush hour; use Gett app for fixed airport rates 15 % lower.
- Store a small snack in your carry-on; post-security food choices shrink after 10 p.m.

## Terminals & Navigation
- All international flights use Terminal 3; the walk from train station to check-in is 8 minutes.
- Terminal 1 sits 1.5 km away—use the free shuttle only if your airline explicitly lists it.
- Gates are numbered 1–15 (domestic) and 21–33 (international); moving walkways cover most distance.
- Allow 15 minutes to clear the final passport control when walking to gates.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- The Dan Lounge in Terminal 3 (after security) offers decent hot food and showers for $45 day pass.
- Aroma and Roladin in the public hall serve reliable coffee and sandwiches before security.
- Quiet seating is limited; the far end of gate area 30 usually has empty rows after 9 p.m.
- Free water fountains exist near every restroom; paid bottles cost 12 ₪ post-security.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- High-speed train is fastest to Tel Aviv; buy a ticket at the machine to avoid lines.
- Shared Nesher vans to Jerusalem cost 89 ₪ and leave every hour from the arrivals level.
- Short-term parking (P1) is 25 ₪ per hour; long-term (P3) drops to 60 ₪ per day after the first 24 hours.
- Uber and Gett pick-up is on level -1; do not use the official taxi queue if your phone works.
