# WUH Airport Guide

> Fastest path through Wuhan’s single dominant terminal with minimal security delays and reliable metro access.

## Quick Facts
- T3 handles every international flight and nearly all domestic traffic.
- China Southern operates the largest share of gates; Air China and Spring Airlines also based here.
- Metro Line 2 connects directly to Hankou and Wuchang in under an hour for ¥6-8.
- No airside transit between international and domestic without clearing immigration.
- Free Wi-Fi requires Chinese phone number verification; bring a VPN.
- **Power:** Quiet charging room past gate 35 on the right; scarce at other gates.
- **Water:** Refill fountains in west-wing international gates — bring an empty bottle.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Arrive 3 hours early for all international departures; domestic flights need 2.5 hours during peaks.
- Liquids must be in 100 ml containers inside a clear bag; no exceptions for duty-free liquids purchased landside.
- Random explosive trace swabs are common on shoes and laptops—factor in an extra 10 minutes.
- Families and elderly travelers can request the priority lane at the far left of security.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the “Wuhan Airport” WeChat mini-program before arrival for live gate and delay updates.
- Landside Re-Re noodles (stall 1F-12) opens at 06:00 and closes at 22:00; portions are large and cheap.
- If your flight is delayed more than 90 minutes, exit security and re-enter; the queue is shorter than waiting airside.
- Metro vending machines on the platform sell single-journey tokens; no need to queue at the counter.
- T3 has free luggage wrapping stations near check-in rows 10-12—use them before international flights.
- Power outlets are scarce airside; the quiet charging room is past gate 35 on the right.
- Taxi stands are on the lowest level; insist on the meter or use Didi app to avoid overcharging.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T3 is a single long building; walking time from security to farthest gate is 12-15 minutes.
- Free inter-terminal shuttle stops only at T3 doors 1 and 9—avoid if your flight is in T3.
- International gates are concentrated in the west wing; follow red signs after immigration.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- China Southern Lounge (T3 airside, near gate 28) offers decent hot food and showers; access with business class or SkyTeam Elite+.
- The best quiet seating is the row of chairs between gates 42-46; few announcements and strong Wi-Fi.
- Avoid the overpriced Starbucks airside; the Luckin Coffee landside is half the price and faster.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Metro Line 2 is the cheapest and most reliable option; trains run 06:00-23:00.
- Taxi to downtown costs ¥80-110 and takes 45-60 minutes depending on traffic; use the official rank.
- Short-term parking is ¥10 per hour in P1/P2; long-term P3 is ¥40 per day but requires a 10-minute walk.
